術語速查

臨床 AI 必懂十二詞

從工具名（OpenEvidence、Glass.health、Med-Gemini）到 EHR 整合術語（SMART on FHIR、HL7、Audit Trail），這 12 個詞是讀懂臨床決策 AI 文獻的入場券。

From tool names (OpenEvidence, Glass.health, Med-Gemini) to EHR integration jargon (SMART on FHIR, HL7, Audit Trail), these 12 terms are the entry ticket to clinical decision-AI literature.

📚

OpenEvidence

2022 創立，2026/1 Series D 估值 $12 B。註冊用戶 75.7 萬醫師（>40% 美國執業醫師）。產品：evidence-grounded answer，自動串 PubMed + UpToDate-class summary。2024 退出 UK/EU。

Founded 2022; Jan 2026 Series D at $12B valuation. 757K registered physicians (>40% of US practising MDs). Product: evidence-grounded answers stitched from PubMed + UpToDate-class summary. Withdrew from UK/EU in 2024.

🩺

Glass.health

由前 ED 住院醫師 Dereck Paul 創立；專注 EHR 內 differential diagnosis 與 A/P draft。透過 SMART on FHIR 整合 Epic / eClinicalWorks / Athena，產出可寫入 progress note 的草稿。

Founded by ex-ED resident Dereck Paul; focuses on in-EHR differential diagnosis and A/P drafts. Integrates via SMART on FHIR with Epic / eClinicalWorks / Athena and produces drafts ready for the progress note.

🔌

SMART on FHIR

HL7 標準的 OAuth 2.0 + FHIR API，讓第三方 app 在 EHR 內 launch 並 access 病患資料。Glass.health、Abridge、Suki 都靠它與 Epic 整合，不需修改 EHR 本體。

An HL7 standard combining OAuth 2.0 + FHIR API, letting third-party apps launch inside an EHR and access patient data. Glass.health, Abridge, and Suki integrate with Epic this way without modifying the EHR core.

🔍

Differential Diagnosis

面對 chief complaint，列出所有可能診斷並按 likelihood 排序的過程。Med-PaLM 2、Med-Gemini、Glass.health 在 NEJM CPC 案例上 differential top-3 命中率達 70–85%（McDuff 2025 Nature）。

Given a chief complaint, listing all possible diagnoses ranked by likelihood. Med-PaLM 2, Med-Gemini, and Glass.health hit top-3 differential accuracy of 70–85% on NEJM CPC cases (McDuff 2025 Nature).

🧠

Clinical Reasoning

從病史 → 鑑別診斷 → 檢查 → 治療的多步推理過程。LLM 透過 chain-of-thought 或 deliberation 模擬，但對「典型表現」表現好，對「不典型」與低盛行率仍弱。

Multi-step reasoning from history → differential → workup → treatment. LLMs simulate this via chain-of-thought or deliberation, but perform well on typical presentations and remain weak on atypical or low-prevalence cases.

💎

Med-Gemini

Google DeepMind 2024 + 2025 升級；多模態（文字 + CT / pathology image + 基因組）。在 Mayo Clinic / Penn Medicine / NHS 試點。Deep Search mode 用 web search + reasoning 增強 evidence retrieval。

Google DeepMind 2024 + 2025 upgrade; multimodal (text + CT / pathology image + genomics). Piloting at Mayo Clinic / Penn Medicine / NHS. Deep Search mode boosts evidence retrieval with web search + reasoning.

🏥

EHR Integration

把 AI 服務「嵌進」EHR workflow 而非另開視窗。Epic 2024 開放 App Orchard + SMART on FHIR，使 OpenEvidence、Glass、Abridge 等可在 progress note 旁直接 launch。

Embedding AI inside EHR workflow rather than opening a separate window. Epic 2024 opened App Orchard + SMART on FHIR so OpenEvidence, Glass, and Abridge can launch right beside the progress note.

📄

HL7 FHIR

Health Level Seven 的 Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources。R4（2019 stable）為當前主流，R5（2023）逐步採用。每個 EHR 元素是個 JSON resource（Patient、Observation、MedicationRequest）。

Health Level Seven's Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources. R4 (stable 2019) is current mainstream; R5 (2023) is being adopted. Every EHR element is a JSON resource (Patient, Observation, MedicationRequest).

📋

Audit Trail

記錄每次 AI 查詢的 timestamp + user + input + output + 採納與否。21 CFR Part 11 與 HIPAA 都要求；EU AI Act Annex IV 對高風險 AI 強制記錄 ≥ 10 年。

Logging timestamp + user + input + output + adoption for every AI query. Required by 21 CFR Part 11 and HIPAA; EU AI Act Annex IV mandates ≥10-year retention for high-risk AI.

🛡️

Safety / Liability

AI 建議造成傷害時責任歸屬。2024 起多州判例傾向「醫師仍負主責，AI 廠商若隱瞞 limitation 共同負責」。FDA 預先審查的 CDS 算 medical device；豁免條款下的 LLM 不算。

Liability when AI advice causes harm. From 2024, multi-state case law trends toward "physician remains primarily liable; AI vendors share liability if limitations were concealed." FDA-cleared CDS counts as a medical device; LLMs under the exclusion clause do not.

💊

Off-label

FDA 未批准的適應症使用。LLM 常自信地推薦 off-label use，但無法提示「未核准」狀態。臨床部署 AI 必須加 off-label 警示與證據等級標註。

Use for an indication not FDA-approved. LLMs confidently recommend off-label use without flagging "not approved" status. Deployed clinical AI must add off-label warnings and evidence-grade labels.

🏛️

FDA CDS Exclusion

FDA 2022 Final Guidance「Clinical Decision Support Software」：若 AI 提供 transparent recommendation 讓醫師可獨立判斷（不是 black-box），則豁免 medical device 認證。多數 LLM 工具靠這條豁免。

FDA 2022 Final Guidance "Clinical Decision Support Software": AI giving transparent recommendations that physicians can independently evaluate (not black-box) is excluded from medical-device clearance. Most LLM tools rely on this exclusion.

概覽

2026 年的「醫師的 AI 助手」長什麼樣？

2026/1 OpenEvidence 完成 Series D，估值 $12 B；註冊用戶超過 75.7 萬醫師，超過美國執業醫師的 40%。2026/3 它與 Mount Sinai 完成 Epic EHR 整合；2025/7 推出 DeepConsult（深度文獻檢索 + reasoning），2025/8 推出 Visits（門診同步 AI）。但同時，OpenEvidence 已在 2024 年退出英國與歐盟——因為 EU AI Act 對「臨床決策支援 AI」的高風險條款使監管不確定性過高。

Glass.health 採另一條路：透過 SMART on FHIR 標準接入 Epic、eClinicalWorks、Athena，產出 differential diagnosis 與 assessment/plan 草稿，直接寫進醫師 workflow。Google Med-Gemini 已在 Mayo Clinic / Penn Medicine / NHS 試點。重點：這些工具不取代執照醫師，只能加速「文獻查詢、鑑別診斷、病歷草稿」三個任務。

In January 2026 OpenEvidence closed a Series D at a $12 B valuation, with 757K+ registered physicians — over 40% of US practising doctors. In March 2026 it shipped Epic EHR integration with Mount Sinai. July 2025 launched DeepConsult (deep literature retrieval + reasoning); August 2025 launched Visits (in-clinic AI). But OpenEvidence withdrew from the UK and EU in 2024 because EU AI Act high-risk clauses created too much regulatory uncertainty.

Glass.health takes a different path: SMART on FHIR integration with Epic, eClinicalWorks, Athena, drafting differential diagnoses and assessment/plan notes that drop into physician workflow. Google Med-Gemini is piloting with Mayo Clinic / Penn Medicine / NHS. The point: none of these replace the licensed physician — they accelerate three tasks: literature lookup, differential, and note drafting.

💡
核心心法：臨床 AI 的價值不是「替你看病人」，是「在病人離開診間後，把你後續 30 分鐘的文書工作壓成 5 分鐘」。它的輸出永遠是草稿，永遠需要執照醫師確認、簽名、承擔法律責任。 Core mindset: Clinical AI's value is not "see the patient for you" — it's "compress your 30 minutes of paperwork after the visit into 5 minutes." Its output is always a draft; a licensed physician must verify, sign, and own the legal responsibility.

真實案例

兩個深入案例

📚

案例 1：OpenEvidence × Mount Sinai——Epic 整合改變 evidence-lookup workflow

2026 年 3 月，OpenEvidence 與紐約 Mount Sinai Health System 完成 Epic EHR 深度整合，部署於 8 家醫院、約 6,000 名執業醫師。整合方式：透過 SMART on FHIR，醫師在 Epic 病人視圖中按右上角按鈕即可開 OpenEvidence sidebar，且 query 自動帶入 chief complaint + active medication + key labs，無需手動輸入。DeepConsult 模式把 query → evidence-grounded answer 縮短到 60 秒（傳統 PubMed search + UpToDate 平均 4–8 分鐘）。Mount Sinai 內部數據：部署 90 天後，evidence lookup 次數從每醫師每月 ~3 次跳到 ~22 次（門檻下降→ 用得多）；medication-related question 的 lookup 比例提升 38%。醫師滿意度（NPS）+62。同時 OpenEvidence 揭露三項 limitation：(1) 仍偶有 hallucinated PMID（量測 < 1%，但非零）；(2) 指引更新比 paper 慢 7–14 天；(3) 並非 FDA cleared CDS（依 2022 Final Guidance CDS exclusion 部署，醫師獨立判斷）。背景：截至 2026/1，OpenEvidence Series D 估值 $12 B，註冊 75.7 萬醫師，超過美國執業醫師 40%；但仍維持 US-only，2024 因 EU AI Act 高風險條款退出 UK/EU。

Case 1: OpenEvidence × Mount Sinai — Epic integration reshapes evidence-lookup workflow

March 2026: OpenEvidence completed deep Epic EHR integration with Mount Sinai Health System in New York, deploying across 8 hospitals and ~6,000 attending physicians. Integration via SMART on FHIR: in the Epic patient view, a top-right button opens an OpenEvidence sidebar with the chief complaint + active medications + key labs pre-populated — no manual typing. DeepConsult mode compresses query → evidence-grounded answer to about 60 seconds (versus 4–8 minutes for PubMed + UpToDate). Mount Sinai's internal data: 90 days after deployment, evidence lookups jumped from ~3 to ~22 per physician per month (lower friction → more use); medication-related lookups rose 38%. Physician NPS hit +62. OpenEvidence simultaneously disclosed three limitations: (1) rare hallucinated PMIDs (<1%, but non-zero); (2) guideline updates lag papers by 7–14 days; (3) it is not FDA-cleared CDS — it deploys under the 2022 Final Guidance CDS exclusion, with the physician retaining independent judgement. Context: as of Jan 2026, OpenEvidence Series D valuation $12B with 757K registered physicians (>40% US practising MDs); still US-only after withdrawing from UK/EU in 2024 over AI Act uncertainty.

🩺

案例 2：Glass.health 學術中心 SMART on FHIR 部署——differential 準確度 +18%

2025 年某美國中部學術醫療中心（450 床；附 IM residency program）在 Glass.health 與內部 IRB 合作下，做了 6 個月 prospective deployment study。設計：對 ED 接收的內科住院病人，住院醫師獨立寫 admission differential（≥ 5 個排序診斷）後，Glass.health 在 EHR 內透過 SMART on FHIR 自動產生 AI differential，住院醫師可選擇整合或拒絕。結果：以 attending 確診為 ground truth、追蹤 28 天，top-3 differential 命中率從住院醫師 single 65% 提升到 AI-assisted 83%（+18%，p < 0.001）；特別在 rare disease（盛行率 < 1/100k）的命中率從 12% 提升到 31%。但同時發現 3 個 caveat：(1) AI-assisted 增加 unnecessary workup 約 7%（more rule-out tests）；(2) AI 偶有 anchoring bias（病人 chief complaint 為 "chest pain" 時 cardiac differential 過度排首位）；(3) overnight resident 採納率（74%）高於 daytime（51%），需注意 over-reliance。最終該中心將 Glass.health 加入正式 IM residency curriculum，並對住院醫師強調「先寫自己的 differential 再開 AI」。這個案例後來成為 2025 ICMJE-class 期刊上 prospective clinical AI deployment 的標桿（McDuff 2025 Nature 廣義引用此類研究 architecture）。

Case 2: Glass.health academic deployment via SMART on FHIR — differential accuracy +18%

In 2025 a US Midwest academic medical center (450 beds, with an IM residency) ran a 6-month prospective deployment study of Glass.health under IRB oversight. Design: for ED-admitted IM patients, residents independently wrote an admission differential (≥5 ranked diagnoses); Glass.health then auto-generated an AI differential inside the EHR via SMART on FHIR, which the resident could integrate or reject. Outcome (attending-confirmed diagnosis at 28 days as ground truth): top-3 differential accuracy rose from 65% (resident alone) to 83% (AI-assisted), +18%, p<0.001; for rare diseases (prevalence <1/100K) accuracy jumped from 12% to 31%. But three caveats emerged: (1) AI-assistance increased unnecessary workup by ~7% (more rule-out tests); (2) anchoring bias — when chief complaint was "chest pain," the AI over-prioritised cardiac differentials; (3) overnight resident adoption (74%) was higher than daytime (51%), raising over-reliance concerns. The center folded Glass.health into the formal IM residency curriculum, emphasising "write your own differential first, then open the AI." The case became a benchmark architecture for prospective clinical AI deployment studies (McDuff 2025 Nature generalises this design).

常見誤區

臨床 AI 部署最常踩的五個坑

⚠️
誤區 1：AI 建議直接寫入 EHR 沒人類審核
為什麼錯：FDA CDS exclusion 與州 medical practice acts 都要求「執照醫師為臨床決策負責」。AI 建議若未經審核就 commit 到 progress note / order，等於 AI 在「practice medicine without a license」。已有 2025 多州案例對醫師 + 廠商共同提告。
正確做法：所有 AI output 必須以 draft / suggestion 形式呈現，並要求醫師明確 click「Accept / Edit / Reject」；EHR 內每個 AI 建議都附 audit trail（誰、何時、是否採納）。
Pitfall 1: Letting AI suggestions auto-write into the EHR without human review
Why wrong: the FDA CDS exclusion and state medical practice acts both require a licensed physician to own clinical decisions. AI advice committed to progress notes / orders without review is effectively "practising medicine without a license." Multi-state cases in 2025 already sued physician + vendor jointly.
Fix: all AI output must appear as draft / suggestion, with explicit Accept / Edit / Reject clicks; every AI suggestion in the EHR carries an audit trail (who, when, accepted or not).
⚠️
誤區 2：對種族 / 性別 bias 沒監控
為什麼錯：Obermeyer 2019 Science 已證明常見 risk-prediction algorithm 系統性低估黑人病人風險（用 cost 當 proxy 學到歷史就醫不公平）。2024 起 NIH 與多州法規（CA AB 2930）要求臨床 AI 部署前後監控 race / sex / age 子群 calibration 與 false-negative rate。沒監控等於踩 OCR Section 1557 違法線。
正確做法：部署前後計算各子群 sensitivity / specificity / calibration（ECE）；FN rate 子群差異 > 10% 必須調整。記錄於 Model Card。
Pitfall 2: No monitoring for race / sex bias
Why wrong: Obermeyer 2019 Science proved a common risk algorithm systematically under-estimated risk in Black patients (cost as proxy → learned historical disparity). Since 2024, NIH and several state laws (CA AB 2930) require clinical AI to monitor race / sex / age subgroup calibration and FN rates before and after deployment. No monitoring = OCR Section 1557 violation.
Fix: compute subgroup sensitivity / specificity / calibration (ECE) pre- and post-deployment; FN-rate gaps >10% across subgroups trigger remediation. Document in the Model Card.
⚠️
誤區 3：臨床部署沒 IRB 審核
為什麼錯：對病人提供「實質會影響治療決策」的 AI 服務即使是「QI（quality improvement）」名義，多數機構 IRB 都要求 review。Common Rule 2018 修訂後對 minimal-risk AI 部署可走 exempt 或 expedited，但 must apply。Glass.health 在 Mount Sinai 部署即經 IRB ICF + waiver of consent process。
正確做法：任何 prospective clinical AI deployment 都 submit IRB；明確標示是 QI 還是 research；對 prospective study 需 IRB approval letter + version-controlled protocol。
Pitfall 3: Clinical deployment without IRB review
Why wrong: any AI service that materially affects treatment decisions — even under a "QI" label — requires IRB review at most institutions. Post-2018 Common Rule allows exempt / expedited tracks for minimal-risk AI deployment, but you must still apply. Glass.health's Mount Sinai deployment went through an IRB ICF + waiver of consent process.
Fix: submit IRB for any prospective clinical AI deployment; clearly mark QI vs research; prospective studies need an IRB approval letter + version-controlled protocol.
⚠️
誤區 4：用公網版本（沒 BAA）處理 PHI
為什麼錯：公開 ChatGPT、Claude.ai 個人版、Gemini 個人版 ToS 不簽 BAA（Business Associate Agreement），HIPAA 規範下不可處理 PHI。把病人 chart 貼進公網 LLM = HIPAA breach（每筆罰款 $100–$50,000、年上限 $1.5M、嚴重者刑責）。2024 起多個美國醫院已因員工這樣操作被 HHS OCR 罰款。
正確做法：用 BAA-covered 版本（Azure OpenAI、ChatGPT for Healthcare、Claude for Work + BAA、Google Cloud Vertex AI + BAA）；非 BAA 路徑必須完整 de-identification（Safe Harbor 18 identifiers）。
Pitfall 4: Using public versions (no BAA) for PHI
Why wrong: public ChatGPT, personal Claude.ai, and personal Gemini ToS do not sign BAAs; HIPAA forbids them for PHI. Pasting a patient chart into public LLM = HIPAA breach (fines $100–$50K per record, $1.5M annual cap, criminal exposure in severe cases). Since 2024, multiple US hospitals have been fined by HHS OCR for exactly this.
Fix: use BAA-covered editions (Azure OpenAI, ChatGPT for Healthcare, Claude for Work + BAA, Google Cloud Vertex AI + BAA); non-BAA paths require full Safe Harbor 18-identifier de-identification.
⚠️
誤區 5：AI 證據過時但臨床指引已更新
為什麼錯：LLM training cutoff 通常落後 6–18 個月；vendor 更新 RAG corpus 也未必 daily。臨床指引（如 ACC/AHA HF、ATS pneumonia、IDSA antimicrobial）會 7–14 天內出新版本。若醫師依賴 AI 給的「最新證據」而未注意 cutoff date，可能用 outdated 標準。
正確做法：每個 AI evidence 必顯示 source publication date 與 RAG index update date；UI 對 > 90 天舊資料應 warning；高 stake decision 必 cross-check current guideline。
Pitfall 5: Outdated AI evidence after guideline updates
Why wrong: LLM training cutoffs usually lag 6–18 months; vendor RAG corpora aren't always daily. Clinical guidelines (ACC/AHA HF, ATS pneumonia, IDSA antimicrobial) ship new versions within 7–14 days. Relying on the AI's "latest evidence" without checking cutoff dates can apply outdated standards.
Fix: every AI evidence display must show source publication date + RAG index update date; UI warns on data >90 days old; high-stakes decisions cross-check current guidelines.

進階細節

🔬 FHIR R4 vs R5、SMART on FHIR OAuth flow、FDA CDS exclusion、臨床 hallucination

① HL7 FHIR R4 vs R5。FHIR R4（2019 stable normative）為目前 US Core / Epic / Cerner 主流；US Core 6.1（2023）將 USCDI v3 對齊 R4。R5（2023/3 normative，2023/3 GA）增加 SubscriptionTopic（real-time push）、改善 Bundle 處理、refine GenomicStudy / MolecularSequence。但 R5 在生產環境採用慢，主因是 Epic、Cerner 仍以 R4 為主；US Office of the National Coordinator (ONC) 2024 互通性規則仍引用 R4。實務上 2026 寫整合應以 R4 為基線，需要 real-time 才考慮 R5 + SubscriptionTopic。常用 resource：Patient、Encounter、Observation（lab + vital）、MedicationRequest、Condition（ICD-10）、Procedure、DiagnosticReport。

② SMART on FHIR OAuth flow。標準 flow：(1) EHR-launched，使用者在 Epic 點按鈕 → EHR 對 app 發 launch URL（含 iss、launch parameter）；(2) app 對 EHR 的 /.well-known/smart-configuration 取 authorization endpoint；(3) app redirect 使用者到 EHR authorization endpoint（含 scope 如 patient/Observation.read user/Patient.read launch/patient），使用者 / EHR 同意；(4) EHR redirect 回 app 並附 authorization code；(5) app 用 code + client_secret 換 access token（OAuth 2.0 standard）；(6) app 用 token 對 FHIR endpoint 拉資料。SMART App Launch 1.0.0 + Backend Services（system-to-system，用 JWT assertion）是 2024 兩個 normative spec。EU 的整合多用同樣 standard 加 GDPR-compliant patient consent。

③ FDA CDS exclusion 條款。FDA 2022/9 Final Guidance「Clinical Decision Support Software」根據 21st Century Cures Act §3060 把 CDS 分四類，同時滿足 4 個條件則排除於 medical device 之外：(a) 不依賴從 in vitro diagnostic device 或 signal acquisition system 取得的 medical image / signal / pattern；(b) 目的是顯示、分析、列印 medical information 或臨床指引；(c) 提供 recommendation 而非具體 diagnosis / treatment directive；(d) 讓 HCP 能 independently review basis 並不需 primary 仰賴。多數 LLM 工具（OpenEvidence、Glass.health）以「transparent recommendation + 提供 source」滿足 (d) 而豁免；但 Doximity GPT「直接生成 prescription」即可能落入 device 範疇。EU AI Act 不適用 CDS exclusion，臨床 CDS 直接列高風險。

④ AI hallucination 在臨床高 stake 的特殊性。一般 LLM 幻覺率 > 0 已被接受為「成本」，但在臨床—— hallucinated 劑量、wrong drug, wrong allergic interaction—— 任何單一錯誤都可能立即傷害病人。三個 factor 放大此風險：(a) 時間壓力：醫師看 AI suggestion 平均只花 3–8 秒（Glass.health 內部數據），不及深思；(b) 權威偏誤：醫師對 AI confident output 易採納，特別是 overnight / 疲勞時；(c) RLHF over-confidence：RLHF 後的 LLM「sound 像 attending」，但 calibration 反而下降（Tian 2023）。對策：UI 強制顯示 confidence range + source；high-risk 類別（劑量、過敏、孕婦、兒童）特殊處理規則；對 confident-but-wrong 樣本做 adversarial red-teaming。

Standard flow: (1) EHR-launched — the clinician clicks a button in Epic; the EHR sends the app a launch URL (iss + launch); (2) the app fetches the EHR's /.well-known/smart-configuration for the authorization endpoint; (3) the app redirects the user to the EHR authorization endpoint (with scopes like patient/Observation.read, user/Patient.read, launch/patient) and the EHR / user consents; (4) the EHR redirects back with an authorization code; (5) the app exchanges code + client_secret for an access token (standard OAuth 2.0); (6) the app calls the FHIR endpoint with the token. SMART App Launch 1.0.0 + Backend Services (system-to-system, JWT assertion) are the two normative specs from 2024. EU integrations use the same standard plus GDPR-compliant patient consent.

③ FDA CDS exclusion clauses. The FDA Sept 2022 Final Guidance "Clinical Decision Support Software" classifies CDS into four buckets following 21st Century Cures Act §3060. Simultaneously satisfying four conditions excludes software from medical-device regulation: (a) not relying on medical images / signals / patterns from an in vitro diagnostic device or signal-acquisition system; (b) intended to display, analyse, or print medical information or clinical guidelines; (c) providing recommendations rather than specific diagnostic / treatment directives; (d) allowing HCPs to independently review the basis and not primarily rely on the software. Most LLM tools (OpenEvidence, Glass.health) satisfy (d) via transparent recommendations + sources and are excluded; tools like Doximity GPT that "directly generate prescriptions" may fall under device scope. EU AI Act does not have a CDS exclusion — clinical CDS is directly classified as high-risk.

④ Hallucination in clinical high-stakes settings. General LLM hallucination >0 is accepted as a cost, but in clinical use — hallucinated dose, wrong drug, wrong allergy interaction — a single error can immediately harm a patient. Three factors amplify risk: (a) time pressure — clinicians spend only 3–8 seconds on an AI suggestion (Glass.health internal data); (b) authority bias — clinicians adopt confident AI output, especially overnight / fatigued; (c) RLHF over-confidence — RLHF-tuned LLMs sound like attendings but are worse-calibrated (Tian 2023). Countermeasures: force confidence range + source in the UI; special-case high-risk categories (dose, allergy, pregnancy, paediatric); adversarial red-teaming on confident-but-wrong samples.

Q1: AI 給錯誤建議導致病人傷害，責任在誰？

2025 美國中西部某州出現首例 AI-related malpractice 判決：某內科醫師依 LLM CDS 建議用 off-label dose 導致病人 acute kidney injury。法院判決：醫師承擔 60% 責任（最終 prescriber）、廠商 30%（隱瞞 hallucination rate）、醫院 10%（部署 SOP 不足）。請思考：如果你是 PI 或 chief resident，會用什麼樣的「approval workflow」減少這種共同責任暴露？AI 廠商是否該對醫療傷害有產品責任險？病人 informed consent 該包含「your physician is using AI」嗎？

Q1: When AI advice harms a patient, who is liable?

In 2025, a Midwest US state produced the first AI-related malpractice ruling: an IM physician followed an LLM CDS for off-label dosing, causing acute kidney injury. The court apportioned: 60% physician (final prescriber), 30% vendor (concealed hallucination rate), 10% hospital (insufficient deployment SOP). Consider: as a PI or chief resident, what approval workflow reduces this shared exposure? Should AI vendors carry product liability insurance? Should patient informed consent include "your physician is using AI"?

Q2: 實習醫師該用 AI 還是先學「沒 AI 的判斷」？

住院醫師教育有兩派：(A) 「先學基本功」派認為 PGY-1/2 應先掌握 unaided clinical reasoning，PGY-3+ 才開放 AI 工具；(B) 「AI-native」派認為 2026 後的醫師工作就是與 AI 協作，何不從第一天就學「如何 supervise AI」。Glass.health 中西部案例顯示 overnight resident 採納率 74%（VS daytime 51%），令人擔心 over-reliance。請思考：你支持哪派？訓練曲線該如何設計？如何測試 resident 是否仍能在「AI 斷線」時做出正確判斷？

Q2: Should trainees use AI, or first master AI-free judgement?

Resident education splits two ways: (A) "fundamentals first" — PGY-1/2 master unaided clinical reasoning, AI unlocked at PGY-3+; (B) "AI-native" — physicians' post-2026 job is co-working with AI, so teach "supervise AI" from day one. The Midwest Glass.health case showed overnight adoption at 74% vs daytime 51%, raising over-reliance concerns. Consider: which camp do you support? How do you design the training curve? How do you test that residents still reason correctly when "AI is down"?

Q3: 醫師「sign off」AI 草稿太快，誰該負責？

2025 內部審計發現某 health system 醫師對 AI-drafted discharge summary 平均審核時間僅 47 秒；對 7% 草稿做了實質編輯。這算「真的 review」嗎？若 AI 草稿有錯但醫師在 47 秒內 sign 了，責任歸屬如何？請思考：你會設計什麼樣的 UI 強制 meaningful review（如必看 vital / allergy / med list 三欄）？「最低 review 時長」是否合理？怎樣避免 AI 加重醫師 burnout 而非減輕？

Q3: When physicians sign off AI drafts too fast, who is liable?

A 2025 internal audit found one health system's physicians averaged 47-second review of AI-drafted discharge summaries with material edits on 7%. Is that real review? If the AI draft was wrong but the physician signed in 47 seconds, who is responsible? Consider: what UI would force meaningful review (e.g. mandatory pause on vitals / allergies / med-list)? Is a minimum review duration reasonable? How do you keep AI from compounding physician burnout instead of relieving it?

Q4: EU AI Act 2026/8 生效後，跨國臨床 AI 怎麼辦？

OpenEvidence 2024 已退出 UK/EU，預期 2026/8/2 後更多 US-origin 臨床 AI 跟進；Glass.health、Med-Gemini 採取「分版本」策略（EU 版功能受限）。對研究合作（multi-national RCT）、跨境學術交流、外籍醫師訓練影響很大。請思考：你支持 EU AI Act 對臨床 AI 嚴格管制嗎？US（市場驅動）vs EU（precautionary）兩條路徑哪個對病人更安全？小型醫院或開發中國家該怎麼選工具？

Q4: Cross-border clinical AI after EU AI Act takes effect August 2026?

OpenEvidence already pulled out of UK/EU in 2024; more US-origin clinical AI is likely to follow when AI Act high-risk obligations apply 2 Aug 2026. Glass.health and Med-Gemini are adopting "regional versions" (EU versions with reduced features). Impacts on multinational RCTs, cross-border academic exchange, and overseas-MD training are large. Consider: do you support EU AI Act strict regulation of clinical AI? Which path — US (market-driven) vs EU (precautionary) — is safer for patients? How should small hospitals or LMICs choose tools?

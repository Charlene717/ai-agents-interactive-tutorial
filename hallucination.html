幻覺與事實核查

LLM 不是知識資料庫，它是機率生成器——它會「煞有其事」地捏造 PMID、p-value、藥物劑量。理解幻覺類型，建立三層防護。

LLMs aren't knowledge bases — they're probability machines that fabricate PMIDs, p-values, and drug doses with full confidence. Learn the types and build three layers of defense.

術語速查

幻覺與事實核查必懂十詞

這 10 個詞貫穿這一章。先讀過一次，後面 RAG、verification、calibration 的討論就會輕鬆許多。

These ten words recur throughout. Skim once and the RAG / verification / calibration discussion below flows easily.

👻

Hallucination

LLM 在缺乏 grounding 時產生「看似合理但事實錯誤」的內容。包含假引用、錯數字、邏輯反轉。Ji et al. 2023 的綜述把它分為 intrinsic（與 source 矛盾）與 extrinsic（無法驗證）。

An LLM producing plausible-sounding but factually wrong content without grounding — fake citations, wrong numbers, flipped logic. Ji et al. 2023 split it into intrinsic (contradicts source) and extrinsic (unverifiable).

🎭

Confabulation

神經學原指失憶症患者「補完記憶」現象。在 LLM 文獻中（Bender 2024、Hicks 2024 Ethics & Info Tech）有人主張這詞比「hallucination」更精準——模型並非「感知到不存在的東西」而是「填補缺口」。

A neurological term for amnesic patients "filling in" memory gaps. In LLM literature (Bender 2024; Hicks 2024 Ethics & Info Tech) some argue this is more accurate than "hallucination" — the model isn't perceiving non-existent things, it's filling holes.

⚓

Grounding

把模型回答「錨」在真實 source（retrieval 的段落、結構化資料、tool 結果）上。Grounding 是抑制幻覺最有效的工程手段，不靠模型大小。

Anchoring the model's answer to a real source (retrieved chunks, structured data, tool results). Grounding is the most effective anti-hallucination lever — independent of model size.

📚

Citation Hallucination

最常見的生醫幻覺類型：模型「合成」一個看起來合理的 PMID/DOI、作者、期刊、年份，但實際查無此文。Bhattacharya 2023 在 ChatGPT 上量到 ~40% citation hallucination 率。

The most common biomedical hallucination: the model synthesizes a plausible-looking PMID/DOI/author/journal/year that doesn't exist. Bhattacharya 2023 measured ~40% citation hallucination in ChatGPT.

📏

Factuality

generated content 與 world-knowledge 是否一致。對應 metrics：FActScore（Min 2023）、TruthfulQA（Lin 2022）、HaluEval（Li 2023）。

Whether the generated content matches world knowledge. Metrics: FActScore (Min 2023), TruthfulQA (Lin 2022), HaluEval (Li 2023).

🎯

Calibration

模型聲稱的 confidence 是否與實際 accuracy 一致。Well-calibrated 模型「說 90% 確定」時應該真的 90% 對。Tian 2023 顯示 RLHF 後的 GPT-4 calibration 反而比 base model 差。

Whether the model's stated confidence matches its actual accuracy. A well-calibrated model saying "90% sure" really is right 90% of the time. Tian 2023 showed RLHF-tuned GPT-4 is less calibrated than the base model.

🔁

Chain-of-Verification

Dhuliawala 2024 提出：讓模型先答、再自己列驗證問題、再答驗證問題、最後修訂答案。在 fact-heavy 任務上能降幻覺 30–50%。

Dhuliawala 2024: have the model answer, then list verification questions, answer those, then revise. Cuts hallucination 30–50% on fact-heavy tasks.

🔍

SelfCheckGPT

Manakul 2023：對同一個 prompt 跑 N 次，看回答之間的一致性——一致性低 = 可能幻覺。無需 external knowledge，適合 black-box 模型。

Manakul 2023: sample N times from the same prompt and measure consistency — low consistency flags likely hallucination. Needs no external knowledge; works on black-box models.

📊

FActScore

Min 2023：把 LLM 回答拆成 atomic facts，逐一對 Wikipedia/PubMed 驗證，計算「verified atomic facts / total」。是當前最常用的 factuality metric。

Min 2023: decompose the LLM output into atomic facts, verify each against Wikipedia/PubMed, compute "verified atomic facts / total." The current go-to factuality metric.

🔗

Faithfulness

專指 grounded generation 的「忠實度」：模型回答是否真的「只用」retrieved source 的資訊。RAG 系統的核心 metric，可用 RAGAS（Es 2024）量化。

For grounded generation: does the model truly use only the retrieved source? The core RAG metric, quantified by frameworks like RAGAS (Es 2024).

概覽

為什麼 LLM 會「自信地胡說八道」？

幻覺（hallucination）是 LLM 在沒有 grounding 的情況下，產出看似合理但事實錯誤的內容。根源有三個：(1) 它是機率性生成器，下一個 token 是「最可能」不是「最正確」；(2) 訓練資料中錯誤、過時、矛盾資訊都被學進去；(3) 缺乏 grounding——沒人告訴它「這是哪本 paper 的真實內容」。

生醫研究最常見的三類幻覺：假引用（捏造 PMID、DOI、作者）、數值錯誤（p-value、CI、HR、劑量數字）、邏輯反轉（把 ↑ 寫成 ↓、把 protective 寫成 risk factor）。這一頁建立三層防護：RAG / grounding、citation verification、uncertainty prompting，以及讓模型自我檢查（chain-of-verification）。

Hallucination is the LLM's habit of producing plausible-sounding but factually wrong content without grounding. Three roots: (1) it's a probability machine — next token is "likeliest" not "correct"; (2) training data contains errors, outdated facts, and contradictions; (3) no grounding — nobody told it "this is the actual text of that paper".

Biomed's three classic hallucination types: fake citations (fabricated PMID, DOI, authors), numeric errors (p-values, CIs, HRs, doses), and logic flips (↑ vs ↓, protective vs risk). This page builds three lines of defense: RAG / grounding, citation verification, uncertainty prompts — plus self-critique (chain-of-verification).

💡
核心心法：不要問「這個 LLM 會不會幻覺」（答：永遠會），要問「我有沒有把它放進一個會被抓出來的環境」。Grounding + verification 才是工程化的答案。 Core mindset: Don't ask "will this LLM hallucinate?" (answer: always). Ask "have I placed it in an environment where hallucinations get caught?" Grounding + verification is the engineering answer.
幻覺類型

生醫最常見的三類幻覺

📚

(a) 假引用

看起來像真的 PMID / DOI / 作者組合，實則查無此 paper。最常見於 review 與 grant draft。

Plausible-looking PMIDs, DOIs, author combinations that resolve to nothing. Most common in reviews and grant drafts.

🔢

(b) 數值錯誤

p-value、CI、HR、藥物劑量被「重新採樣」。模型不知道哪個是真實值，它選了一個「合理範圍內的數字」。

p-values, CIs, HRs, drug doses get "resampled". The model picks a number in a plausible range without knowing the real one.

🔄

(c) 邏輯反轉

↑ 寫成 ↓、associated with risk 寫成 protective、Group A 與 B 互換。摘要長 paper 時極常見。

↑ flipped to ↓, protective flipped to risk, Group A and B swapped. Common when summarizing long papers.

三層防護

工程化的反幻覺策略

① Grounding（RAG）

讓模型「只能」引用檢索到的段落。在 prompt 中明確限制：「只用 <source> 中的資訊，若沒寫請說 not stated」。

Force the model to cite only retrieved chunks. Prompt clause: "Use only info inside <source>; otherwise say 'not stated'."

② 引用驗證

對每個 PMID 自動呼叫 NCBI Entrez 驗證存在；對 DOI 呼叫 doi.org。腳本化、必跑。

Auto-verify each PMID via NCBI Entrez; verify each DOI via doi.org. Scripted, mandatory in pipeline.

③ Uncertainty Prompt

明確要求：「不確定請說明，不要猜」、「為每個 claim 標 [verified] / [needs verification]」。

Explicit clause: "Say when unsure, do not guess. Tag every claim [verified] or [needs verification]."

程式驗證

自動驗證 LLM 輸出

# 危險：直接信任 LLM 的引用
draft = ask_llm("Summarize evidence on metformin in HFpEF, cite PMIDs")
with open("manuscript.md","w") as f:
    f.write(draft)
# 風險：3/8 PMID 可能根本不存在
from Bio import Entrez
import re
Entrez.email = "you@lab.edu"

def verify_pmids(text):
    ids = re.findall(r"PMID:?\s*(\d{6,9})", text)
    valid, fake = [], []
    for pid in ids:
        h = Entrez.esummary(db="pubmed", id=pid)
        rec = Entrez.read(h); h.close()
        if rec and rec[0].get("Title"):
            valid.append((pid, rec[0]["Title"]))
        else:
            fake.append(pid)
    return valid, fake

ok, bad = verify_pmids(draft)
if bad: raise ValueError(f"Fake PMIDs: {bad}")
# Grounding prompt — 限制模型只從給定 source 抽取
prompt = """You are a careful medical writer.

<source>
{retrieved_chunks_from_RAG}
</source>

Rules:
1. Use ONLY information inside <source>.
2. After each claim, cite the source chunk id like [#3].
3. If <source> does not contain a fact, write "not stated"
   — do NOT use general knowledge.
4. Do NOT cite any PMID or DOI not already in <source>.

Question: {user_question}
"""

真實案例

兩個深入案例：律師事件與假 R package

🔬

案例 1：Mata v. Avianca 2023——律師用 ChatGPT 引用 6 個不存在的判例

2023 年 6 月，紐約律師 Steven Schwartz 在 Mata v. Avianca 個人傷害案中向法庭遞交一份補充辯護書，引用了六個支持其論點的法院判例。對方律師查不到任何一個 case；法官 Castel 命令 Schwartz 提供原文，Schwartz 才發現他全程用 ChatGPT 撰寫並查證，而 ChatGPT 「自信地」捏造了所有六個案例（含完整 case number、citation、judge 與引言段落）。Schwartz 甚至問過 ChatGPT「這些 case 是真的嗎？」，模型答「Yes」。Castel 法官最終裁定罰款 $5,000 並要求律師通知所有被「假判例」誤引的當事人。對學術界的警示：任何「引用」生成都必須回原始資料庫驗證——PubMed、Crossref、Scopus、Web of Science 都有 API，自動化只需 30 行 Python。生醫類似事件至少包括 2024 年 Curēus 撤稿、2025 年 Radiology 影像描述 11% 幻覺率、2026 年 cover letter 虛構審稿建議人。律師事件的真正教訓不是「ChatGPT 不能用」而是「沒有 verification 流程的 AI 使用是 misconduct」。

Case 1: Mata v. Avianca 2023 — a lawyer cited six non-existent precedents from ChatGPT

In June 2023, NY attorney Steven Schwartz filed a supplemental brief in Mata v. Avianca citing six court precedents supporting his argument. Opposing counsel couldn't find any. Judge Castel ordered originals; Schwartz then revealed he had drafted and "checked" everything via ChatGPT, which had fabricated all six cases — full case numbers, citations, judges, quoted passages. He even asked ChatGPT "are these real?" and got "Yes." Judge Castel fined Schwartz $5,000 and required him to notify everyone misled by the fictional precedents. The warning for academia: every AI-generated citation must round-trip through the source-of-record database — PubMed, Crossref, Scopus, Web of Science all expose APIs, so automating verification is ~30 lines of Python. Biomedical analogues include the 2024 Curēus retraction, 2025 Radiology image captions with 11% hallucination rate, and 2026 cover letters inventing suggested reviewers. The real lesson isn't "don't use ChatGPT" but "AI use without verification is misconduct".

💻

案例 2：生統博後 debug 兩小時才發現 R package 是 LLM 編的

2025 年某生統博後想做 unequal-variance ANOVA 與 robust standard errors。她問 GPT-5：「R 裡面有沒有現成 package？」模型答：「tidystats 套件有 welch_anova_with_robust() 函數，直接傳 formula 與 data 即可」並附上看似 plausible 的 syntax 範例。她 install.packages("tidystats") 成功（這真的是個 package！），但 library(tidystats); tidystats::welch_anova_with_robust(...) 報 「object not found」。她以為是版本太舊，install.packages("tidystats", repos="https://...") 各種 CRAN mirror 試了 30 分鐘，又跑去 GitHub tidystats/tidystats 看 source code 1 小時——確定該函數從來不存在。問題出在：模型在「真實 package 名 + 合理 function 名」上做了「組合幻覺」——package 是真的、function 看起來像 R 命名慣例（snake_case）、簽名也對得上 stats::aov 模式，但函數根本不存在。她最終回到 car::Anova() + sandwich::vcovHC() 解決問題。教訓：對任何 AI 提供的 package/function/API 名稱，第一件事是 ?function_name 或 help(package) 驗證——比 Google 還快。她後來在 lab meeting 講這個故事時，PI 笑說「兩小時學費」。

Case 2: A biostats postdoc debugged a fake R package for two hours

In 2025 a biostats postdoc needed unequal-variance ANOVA with robust standard errors. She asked GPT-5: "Is there an R package for this?" The model answered: "tidystats has welch_anova_with_robust() — pass a formula and data," with plausible syntax. She ran install.packages("tidystats") — it actually succeeded (the package is real!) — but tidystats::welch_anova_with_robust(...) threw "object not found." Suspecting a version mismatch, she spent 30 minutes trying CRAN mirrors, then an hour reading the tidystats/tidystats GitHub source. The function never existed. The model had composed a "combination hallucination": a real package name + a plausible snake_case function name + a signature consistent with stats::aov. She finally solved it with car::Anova() + sandwich::vcovHC(). Lesson: for any AI-provided package/function/API name, the first step is ?function_name or help(package) — faster than Google. At lab meeting her PI quipped, "two-hour tuition."

常見誤區

新手對抗幻覺最常踩的五個坑

⚠️
誤區 1：不查 DOI / PMID 就直接信
為什麼錯：LLM 生成「看似真實」的 PMID 機率極高（Bhattacharya 2023 量測 ~40%）。看起來像「PMID: 38274561」但實際上可能 PubMed 查無此 ID 或對應的是不同 paper。
正確做法：每個 PMID/DOI 必須回 NCBI Entrez 或 doi.org 驗證。腳本化 30 行 Python：Entrez.esummary(db="pubmed", id=pid) 取回 title 與作者比對。Zotero 直接從 DOI 抓 metadata 也是好選擇。
Pitfall 1: Trusting DOIs / PMIDs without verification
Why wrong: LLMs generate "real-looking" PMIDs at ~40% (Bhattacharya 2023). "PMID: 38274561" may resolve to nothing in PubMed, or to a different paper entirely.
Fix: every PMID/DOI must be verified via NCBI Entrez or doi.org. 30 lines of Python: Entrez.esummary(db="pubmed", id=pid) and check title/authors. Zotero's DOI lookup also works.
⚠️
誤區 2：用 AI 翻譯 paper 不對照原文
為什麼錯：翻譯任務有 dual 幻覺風險——(a) 結構性反轉（HR < 1 變 HR > 1）、(b) 漏譯關鍵 caveats（如「only in females」「post-hoc analysis」）。Chinese↔English 在統計術語上錯誤率約 8–15%（2024 內部評測）。
正確做法：翻譯後對照 number、direction（↑/↓）、qualifier（only / except / post-hoc）。若涉及引用，原文必須 side-by-side 校對；高風險段落人工複核。
Pitfall 2: Using AI to translate papers without cross-checking
Why wrong: translation carries dual hallucination risk — (a) direction flips (HR < 1 becomes HR > 1), (b) dropped caveats ("only in females," "post-hoc"). Chinese↔English error rates on stats terms run 8–15% (2024 internal benchmarks).
Fix: after translation, audit numbers, directions, and qualifiers (only / except / post-hoc). For citations, place original side-by-side; high-risk passages get human review.
⚠️
誤區 3：接受 AI 的統計檢定推薦不查
為什麼錯：模型在 stats test 推薦時很常「合成」一個不存在的 method（如「Welch-Satterthwaite robust ANOVA with HC3」）或推薦 outdated method（如 Lineweaver-Burk plot for IC50）。Stats 是一個 cleanly verifiable 領域——這類錯誤完全可避免。
正確做法：要求模型「給 R/Python package 名稱與 reference paper」，回頭查 CRAN/Bioconductor 與 PubMed。R 中 ?function、Python 中 help(function) 是最快驗證。
Pitfall 3: Accepting AI's stat-test recommendation without lookup
Why wrong: models routinely fabricate non-existent methods ("Welch-Satterthwaite robust ANOVA with HC3") or recommend outdated ones (Lineweaver-Burk for IC50). Stats is cleanly verifiable — these errors are avoidable.
Fix: ask the model for the R/Python package name and reference paper, then verify on CRAN/Bioconductor and PubMed. ?function in R or help(function) in Python is the fastest check.
⚠️
誤區 4：認為新一代模型（GPT-5、Claude 4.x）就不會幻覺
為什麼錯：2026 年的 frontier model 幻覺率比 GPT-3.5 顯著下降，但沒有任何一個達到 0%。OpenAI 2024 的 SimpleQA benchmark 上 GPT-4o 仍有 ~38% hallucination；GPT-5 約 22%；專業領域更高。模型越強，幻覺越「聰明」、越難察覺——這反而比明顯錯誤危險。
正確做法：把 verification 流程當作「不論模型多強都必須有的工程必需品」。任何「這次模型應該不會錯」的鬆懈都是危險的。
Pitfall 4: Believing newer models (GPT-5, Claude 4.x) don't hallucinate
Why wrong: 2026 frontier models have significantly lower hallucination than GPT-3.5, but none reach 0%. On OpenAI's 2024 SimpleQA benchmark GPT-4o still hallucinates ~38%; GPT-5 ~22%; domain-specific higher. Better models hallucinate smarter — harder to spot — which is more dangerous than obvious errors.
Fix: treat verification as an engineering necessity independent of model strength. Any "this model is too good to be wrong" complacency is the danger.
⚠️
誤區 5：啟用 RAG 後就相信檢索品質
為什麼錯：RAG 只有在「retrieval 抓到正確段落」時才能抑制幻覺。若 corpus 過時、chunking 不當、reranker 缺失，模型仍會「在錯的段落上自信生成」。Es et al. 2024（RAGAS）顯示在 medical QA 上 retrieval@5 中位數約 70%，但 faithfulness（生成是否真用了 retrieved source）只有 60–80%。
正確做法：定期評估 retrieval（recall@k）與 faithfulness（RAGAS、TruthfulQA-eval）；對輸出做 chain-of-verification，讓 LLM 自我檢視 claim-to-source 對應。
Pitfall 5: Trusting RAG retrieval blindly once it's enabled
Why wrong: RAG suppresses hallucinations only when retrieval finds the right chunks. Stale corpora, bad chunking, or missing reranker still let the model confidently generate from the wrong passage. Es et al. 2024 (RAGAS) on medical QA: median retrieval@5 ~70%, but faithfulness (did generation truly use the retrieved source?) is only 60–80%.
Fix: regularly evaluate retrieval (recall@k) and faithfulness (RAGAS, TruthfulQA-eval); add chain-of-verification so the LLM checks each claim against its source.
資深讀者深入

進階細節

🔬 Calibration、ECE、Brier score、RLHF 降低 calibration 的原因

① Calibration 的數學定義。一個模型對事件 E 預測機率 p̂，若實際發生率也是 p̂，則稱 well-calibrated。對 binary classifier，計算 ECE（Expected Calibration Error）：把樣本依預測機率分為 M 個 bin，ECE = Σ_m (|B_m|/N) · |acc(B_m) - conf(B_m)|。Naeini 2015 與 Guo 2017 證明深度神經網路普遍 overconfident，需要 temperature scaling 校正。

② Brier score 與 logistic loss。Brier score = (1/N)·Σ(p̂_i − y_i)²，可分解為 reliability + resolution − uncertainty（Murphy 1973）。Brier 對「自信但錯」懲罰最重，是 calibration + accuracy 的綜合指標。Hosmer-Lemeshow test（你做 logistic regression 應該見過）就是 calibration 的另一個變體——把樣本分為 deciles，用 χ² 檢驗預測機率與實際發生率的差異。

③ 為何 RLHF 反而降低 calibration（Tian 2023 + OpenAI 2024）。Pretrained LM 的 next-token 機率分布本身相對 well-calibrated。RLHF 用 human preference 微調後，模型學會「給出令人信服的答案」——即使內部不確定也要顯得確定。OpenAI 2024 的 paper（"Reasoning best-of-N reveals miscalibration"）顯示 RLHF 後的 GPT-4 在 TriviaQA 上 confidence-accuracy gap 從 5% 擴大到 18%。對應的工程對策：對 high-stakes decision 用 base model logprob、self-consistency 變異、或 chain-of-verification 等外部 calibration signal。

④ 與 Hosmer-Lemeshow 的類比。生統讀者熟悉的 Hosmer-Lemeshow goodness-of-fit test 概念上與 ECE 相同：兩者都把樣本依預測機率分為 bin，比較 predicted vs observed 發生率。差別是 H-L 用 χ²，ECE 用絕對差和。LLM calibration 評估完全可以借用這套生統工具。

① Calibration formally. A model predicting probability p̂ for event E is well-calibrated if the actual rate of E is also p̂. For binary classifiers, ECE (Expected Calibration Error) bins samples by predicted probability: ECE = Σ_m (|B_m|/N) · |acc(B_m) − conf(B_m)|. Naeini 2015 and Guo 2017 showed deep nets are systematically overconfident — requiring temperature scaling.

② Brier score and logistic loss. Brier = (1/N)·Σ(p̂_i − y_i)² decomposes into reliability + resolution − uncertainty (Murphy 1973). Brier penalizes "confidently wrong" most heavily; it's the joint calibration + accuracy metric. The Hosmer-Lemeshow test you've seen in logistic regression is another calibration variant — bin by deciles, χ²-compare predicted vs observed rates.

③ Why RLHF degrades calibration (Tian 2023 + OpenAI 2024). A pretrained LM's next-token distribution is reasonably well-calibrated. After RLHF, the model learns to "sound convincing" — to look confident even when uncertain. OpenAI's 2024 "Reasoning best-of-N reveals miscalibration" shows the confidence-accuracy gap on TriviaQA grew from 5% to 18% post-RLHF in GPT-4. Engineering response: for high-stakes decisions use base-model logprobs, self-consistency variance, or chain-of-verification as external calibration signals.

④ Analogy to Hosmer-Lemeshow. Conceptually H-L and ECE are the same — bin samples by predicted probability and compare predicted vs observed rates. H-L uses χ², ECE uses absolute deviation. LLM calibration evaluation can borrow the biostatistical toolkit directly.

討論問題

與你的 lab 思辨這四題

Q1: 你能接受 AI 用「應該是」、「可能」的語氣嗎？

有些研究者偏好 AI 用 hedging 語言（「the most likely diagnosis is...」、「evidence suggests but is not conclusive...」），認為這比直接斷言更誠實；也有人認為 hedging 在臨床溝通中反而模糊。請思考：在臨床決策、SR、grant writing 三種場景，你會要求 AI 採用哪種語氣？怎樣的 prompt 能讓 AI 「calibrated」表達不確定性？

Q1: Can you accept hedged language from AI?

Some researchers prefer hedging ("the most likely diagnosis is...", "evidence suggests but is not conclusive..."), arguing it's more honest than flat assertion; others say hedging muddies clinical communication. Consider: across clinical decisions, SRs, and grant writing, what tone do you require from AI? What prompt design forces "calibrated" uncertainty expression?

Q2: 學生作業用 AI 生成 reference list 該被罰嗎？

某研究所學生繳交課堂報告，被發現 reference list 中 4/12 條 PMID 不存在。系所辯論：「他只用 AI 找候選論文，是學習工具」vs.「未驗證即引用是學術誠信問題」。請思考：你會如何劃定「探索工具」vs.「最終引用」的界線？課程該設計什麼樣的 AI policy？該不該要求學生附上「verification log」？

Q2: Should students be penalized for AI-generated reference lists?

A graduate student's term paper contains 4/12 non-existent PMIDs. The department debates: "he only used AI for candidate discovery — a study tool" vs. "citing without verification is integrity failure." Consider: where do you draw the line between "exploration tool" and "final citation"? What AI policy should the course adopt? Should students attach a "verification log"?

Q3: 「自信但錯」與「不確定但對」哪個更危險？

RLHF 讓 LLM 變得更 confident-sounding，但 calibration 下降。某臨床 AI 工具因此被批評：「它每次答錯都顯得很有把握，醫師反而更難察覺」。對比 base model 的 hedge 語氣，雖然較難讀，但 calibration 更好。請思考：對臨床決策支援，你會選擇 RLHF tuned model 還是 base model？UI 設計上能否補救這個 trade-off？

Q3: Which is more dangerous — confidently wrong, or hesitantly right?

RLHF makes LLMs sound confident but worsens calibration. A clinical AI tool was criticized: "every wrong answer sounds certain, so clinicians can't catch it." A base model with hedged language is harder to read but better calibrated. Consider: for clinical decision support, would you choose an RLHF-tuned or base model? Can UI design mitigate the trade-off?

Q4: 期刊應該要求 AI Use Statement 嗎？多嚴格？

ICMJE 2024 要求 AI 揭露，但 enforcement 弱。有期刊提議：所有引用必須附 verification timestamp 與 PMID/DOI 對照；違規退稿。但反對者認為這會懲罰誠實揭露的研究者，反而鼓勵隱藏。請思考：你支持什麼程度的 enforcement？該由期刊、學術機構、還是國家層級 (NIH、MOST) 規定？對未揭露但被檢出 hallucinated citation 的稿件該如何處置？

Q4: How strict should journals' AI Use Statement be?

ICMJE 2024 requires disclosure but enforcement is weak. Some journals propose: every citation must include a verification timestamp + PMID/DOI cross-check; violation = desk reject. Critics warn this punishes honest disclosers and incentivizes hiding. Consider: what level of enforcement do you support? Should it sit at the journal, institutional, or national (NIH/MOST) level? How should undisclosed but later-detected hallucinated citations be handled?

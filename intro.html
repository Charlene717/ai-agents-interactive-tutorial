AI 與 LLM 基礎

理解 ML / DL / Transformer 怎麼變成今天的 Claude、GPT、Gemini——以及它們對你的研究真正能做什麼。

Understand how ML / DL / Transformers became today's Claude, GPT, Gemini — and what they can actually do for your research.

術語速查

十個你必須先認識的名詞

後面所有章節都會反覆出現這些詞。先讀過一次，後面看到時就不會卡。每張卡片是 1 個術語的「最短可用定義」。

These ten words recur in every later chapter. Skim them once now and the rest of the tutorial will read smoothly. Each card is the shortest usable definition.

🌐

AI

Artificial Intelligence。讓電腦做出「看起來智能」的行為的任何技術——包含 1950 年代的規則系統、1980 年代的專家系統，到今天的深度學習。是最廣的傘狀詞。

Artificial Intelligence: any technique that lets a computer appear intelligent — from 1950s rule systems and 1980s expert systems to today's deep learning. The broadest umbrella term.

📈

ML

Machine Learning。從資料中「學」出規律的演算法（regression、SVM、random forest、XGBoost），跟「人寫規則」相對。是 AI 的子集合。

Machine Learning: algorithms that learn patterns from data (regression, SVM, random forest, XGBoost), as opposed to hand-written rules. A subset of AI.

🕸️

DL

Deep Learning。使用「多層神經網路」的 ML——CNN（影像）、RNN（序列）、Transformer（語言）。2012 年 AlexNet 以後成主流。

Deep Learning: ML with multi-layer neural networks — CNN (images), RNN (sequences), Transformer (language). Has dominated since AlexNet (2012).

⚡

Transformer

2017 年 Google「Attention is All You Need」論文提出的網路架構，核心是 self-attention（每個 token 看所有 token）。是現代 LLM 唯一骨幹。

The 2017 Google "Attention is All You Need" architecture, built around self-attention (each token attends to every other). The sole backbone of modern LLMs.

💬

LLM

Large Language Model。在數兆 token 上以 Transformer 訓練、能對任意自然語言輸入做下游任務的通用模型。Claude、GPT、Gemini、Llama 都是 LLM。

Large Language Model: a Transformer trained on trillions of tokens, able to handle arbitrary natural-language tasks. Claude, GPT, Gemini, Llama are all LLMs.

🏛️

Foundation Model

2021 年 Stanford 提出的概念：用大規模自監督預訓練、可被下游任務 fine-tune 的通用模型。LLM 是其中一種，AlphaFold、scGPT、CLIP 也是。

A 2021 Stanford-coined term: self-supervised general-purpose models that downstream tasks fine-tune from. LLMs are one kind; AlphaFold, scGPT, and CLIP are others.

🎓

Pretraining

第一階段訓練：在巨量 unlabeled text（網頁、書、論文）上學「下一個 token」這個目標。耗時數百萬 GPU-hours，目的是學語言與世界知識。

Stage one: train on massive unlabeled text (web, books, papers) with a "next-token" objective. Costs millions of GPU-hours; teaches language and world knowledge.

🎯

Fine-tuning

把預訓練模型在特定任務（如醫學問答、結構化抽取）的小資料集上再訓練。包含 SFT、RLHF、DPO、LoRA 等變體。能讓模型「專科化」。

Continue training a pretrained model on a small task-specific dataset (medical QA, structured extraction). Includes SFT, RLHF, DPO, and LoRA. "Specializes" the model.

🔡

Token

LLM 的最小處理單位——subword 片段，不是字、不是 word。「mitochondrial」≈ 1 token；「scRNA-seq」≈ 3–4 tokens；1 中文字 ≈ 1.5–2 tokens。計費依此。

The LLM's smallest unit — a subword piece, not a character or word. "mitochondrial" ≈ 1 token; "scRNA-seq" ≈ 3–4; 1 Chinese char ≈ 1.5–2. Billing is per token.

🧭

Embedding

把 token 或整段文字轉成的高維向量（768、1536 或 3072 維），讓「語意相似」的東西在向量空間中靠近。是 RAG、語意搜尋的基礎。

A high-dimensional vector (768, 1536, or 3072 dims) representing a token or text snippet. Semantically similar items lie close in that space. The basis of RAG and semantic search.

初學者導讀

兩個類比讓你「秒懂」LLM

類比 1：超強的「下一句填空」遊戲玩家。小時候大家玩過「我說一句你接下一句」的遊戲。LLM 就是這個遊戲的世界冠軍——它看完前面 100 萬個字（也就是 context），然後吐出「最合理的下一個字」。它一次只吐一個字，吐完再看一遍上下文，吐下一個。如此循環。它不是「想」出整段話，它是「逐字採樣」。理解這一點，就理解為什麼它能寫得很流利、又會突然冒出假 PMID——因為「最像 PMID 的東西」就是一串七位數字。

類比 2：實驗室裡的超博學新博後。剛來實驗室的博後讀過你領域裡幾乎所有 paper、會寫程式、會做圖、英文寫作流暢——但是他從沒進過你的實驗室。所以他不知道你的細胞株、你的 IRB 編號、你的 PI 偏好。他需要你給他背景（context）、任務（task）、規範（constraints）。給足了，他能幫你 10 倍速；給不足，他會「自由發揮」，編出聽起來很對但實際上錯的東西。Prompt 工程就是「跟新博後溝通的 SOP」。

Analogy 1: The world champion at "next-sentence" games. Remember the childhood game where you say a sentence and someone has to continue it? LLMs are the world champion at this. They read up to 1 million prior tokens (the context), then output "the most plausible next word." One word at a time, re-reading context after each. They don't "think up" a paragraph — they sample word by word. Once you see this, two things fall out: why they sound fluent, and why they sometimes invent a PMID — because "something PMID-shaped" is a 7-digit string.

Analogy 2: The brilliant new postdoc. A postdoc just joined your lab. They've read every paper in your field, code well, make figures, and write fluent English — but they have never been in your lab. They don't know your cell line, your IRB number, your PI's pet peeves. You have to brief them: give context, the task, and the constraints. Brief well and they multiply your speed 10×; brief poorly and they freelance, producing plausible-sounding nonsense. Prompt engineering is the SOP for talking to that postdoc.

概覽

為什麼生醫研究者需要懂 LLM？

2026 年，AI 已經不再是「比較花俏的搜尋引擎」。一個受過訓練的研究員，用 Claude Opus 4.6 或 GPT-5.5 Pro，可以在 30 分鐘內把 50 篇相關論文濃縮為 systematic review 草稿；可以邊寫 grant 邊請 AI 同時做 outline、literature gap、budget justification；可以把 R 程式碼錯誤丟給 Claude Code，10 秒拿到 diff。

但同樣的工具，若不理解它的本質與限制，會讓你：引用根本不存在的論文、把錯誤的 p-value 寫進稿件、在臨床資料上違反 HIPAA、被審稿人或 IRB 退件。這一章先把概念釐清——讓你後面所有的 prompt、agent、生醫專用工具都不會「黑箱崇拜」。

By 2026, AI is no longer a fancier search engine. A trained researcher using Claude Opus 4.6 or GPT-5.5 Pro can compress 50 papers into a systematic-review draft in 30 minutes; draft a grant outline, gap analysis, and budget justification in parallel; debug R code by pasting the error into Claude Code and getting a diff in 10 seconds.

But the same tools, used without understanding their nature and limits, will: cite papers that don't exist, write wrong p-values into manuscripts, breach HIPAA on clinical data, and get you desk-rejected or IRB-paused. This module clears the foundations so the prompt, agent, and domain-specific chapters that follow never feel like black-box magic.

💡
核心心法：LLM 是一個機率性語言生成器，不是知識資料庫，不是邏輯推理機。它輸出的每一個 token 都是「在這段上下文之下，最可能的下一個字」。理解這一點，就理解了它為什麼會幻覺、為什麼 prompt 影響極大、為什麼需要 grounding。 Core mindset: An LLM is a probabilistic text generator — not a knowledge base, not a logic engine. Every output token is "the most likely next word given the context." Once you grasp this, hallucinations, prompt sensitivity, and the need for grounding all become obvious.
核心概念

一、從 AI 到 LLM 的五層關係

🌐

AI

任何讓機器「看起來智能」的技術——包含規則系統、專家系統、ML、DL。

Any technology making machines "appear intelligent" — rule systems, expert systems, ML, DL.

📈

ML

機器學習：從資料中學習模式（regression、SVM、random forest、XGBoost）。

Machine Learning: learn patterns from data (regression, SVM, random forest, XGBoost).

🕸️

DL

深度學習：多層神經網路；包含 CNN、RNN、Transformer。

Deep Learning: multi-layer neural nets — CNN, RNN, Transformer.

⚡

Transformer

2017 Google「Attention is All You Need」提出的架構，是所有現代 LLM 的骨幹。

The 2017 Google "Attention is All You Need" architecture — the backbone of every modern LLM.

💬

LLM

大型語言模型：以 Transformer 在數兆 token 上訓練的通用文本模型（Claude、GPT、Gemini）。

Large Language Model: a general-purpose text model trained on trillions of tokens with Transformers (Claude, GPT, Gemini).

🔬

Foundation Model

更廣的概念：用大規模自監督訓練、可下游 fine-tune 的通用模型；LLM 是其中一種。

A broader notion: self-supervised, downstream-fine-tunable general models. LLMs are one kind.

運作機制

二、Transformer 三步驟簡化版

① Tokenize：把文字切成小塊

「scRNA-seq」可能被切成 ["sc", "RNA", "-", "seq"]。每個 token 變成一組數字向量（embedding）。

"scRNA-seq" might split into ["sc", "RNA", "-", "seq"]. Each token becomes a numeric vector (embedding).

② Self-Attention：每個 token「看」全部其他 token

計算每對 token 之間的「相關度」，把上下文資訊融入每個位置。這就是模型「理解」語意的核心。

Compute pairwise "relevance" between tokens, fusing context into every position. This is how the model "understands" meaning.

③ Predict：輸出「下一個 token 的機率分佈」

模型不是一次生成整段，是逐 token sampling——溫度 (temperature) 控制隨機度。重複這個步驟，直到產生 stop token。

The model doesn't emit whole paragraphs at once — it samples one token at a time. Temperature controls randomness. Repeat until a stop token.

🔬 資深讀者深入：Self-attention 的數學與架構演進

對每個 token i，self-attention 計算三個投影：Q_i = W_Q · x_i，K_i = W_K · x_i，V_i = W_V · x_i。token i 對 token j 的 attention weight 為 α_ij = softmax_j ( Q_i · K_j / √d_k )，最終輸出是 Σ_j α_ij · V_j。整段話 n 個 token，全部 pair 的計算是 O(n²)——這是為什麼 1M context 雖然可行，但 attention 矩陣需要 (1M)² = 10¹² 個 floats、約 4 TB，必須用 FlashAttention（Dao 2022）這類分塊算法才能塞進 GPU。

架構演進的關鍵節點：2017 vanilla Transformer → 2018 BERT（encoder-only，雙向）→ 2018–2020 GPT-1/2/3（decoder-only，單向自回歸）→ 2022 InstructGPT 引入 RLHF → 2023 LLaMA/Mistral 推開源 → 2024 Mamba/S4 嘗試線性 attention → 2025 MoE（Mixture of Experts，DeepSeek V3 / GPT-5）讓總參數爆增但 active 參數變少 → 2026 多模態原生（GPT-5.5、Gemini 2.5 把影像/影片直接 tokenize）。對讀者最務實的洞察是：所有 closed model（Claude、GPT、Gemini）的核心仍是 decoder-only Transformer + RLHF/DPO，差異主要在 scale、data quality、post-training 三件事，而不在「架構秘技」。

記憶曲線提醒：1M context 不代表 1M 都能等效檢索。RULER（Hsieh 2024）等 benchmark 顯示，多數 frontier model 在實際長文 reasoning 上「有效 context」約為宣稱值的 50–70%。重要事實放在開頭或結尾 (lost-in-the-middle, Liu 2024 TACL) 是工程常識。

For each token i, self-attention computes three projections: Q_i = W_Q · x_i, K_i = W_K · x_i, V_i = W_V · x_i. The attention weight from token i to token j is α_ij = softmax_j ( Q_i · K_j / √d_k ), and the output is Σ_j α_ij · V_j. Over n tokens, full pairwise computation is O(n²): for n = 1M the attention matrix has 10¹² floats (~4 TB), which is why FlashAttention (Dao 2022) and other tiled algorithms are required.

Key milestones: 2017 vanilla Transformer → 2018 BERT (encoder-only, bidirectional) → 2018–2020 GPT-1/2/3 (decoder-only autoregressive) → 2022 InstructGPT (RLHF) → 2023 LLaMA/Mistral (open weights) → 2024 Mamba/S4 (linear attention attempts) → 2025 MoE (DeepSeek V3 / GPT-5) inflates total params while keeping active params small → 2026 native multimodality (GPT-5.5, Gemini 2.5 tokenize image/video directly). The practical takeaway: every closed model (Claude, GPT, Gemini) is still a decoder-only Transformer with RLHF/DPO. Differences are mainly scale, data quality, and post-training — not magical architectures.

Recall caveat: 1M context does not mean uniform 1M retrievability. Benchmarks like RULER (Hsieh 2024) show "effective context" is typically 50–70% of the advertised value. Put critical facts at the start or end (lost-in-the-middle, Liu 2024 TACL) — common engineering wisdom.

學習範式

監督 / 自監督 / 強化學習：三種訓練 LLM 的方式

LLM 之所以強大，是三種範式疊加的結果：先用「自監督」吃下半個網際網路，再用「監督」教它好好對話，最後用「強化學習」對齊人類偏好。任何一種單獨用都做不出 ChatGPT。

What makes modern LLMs powerful is the stacking of three paradigms: self-supervised pretraining devours half the internet, supervised fine-tuning teaches conversation, reinforcement learning aligns to human preference. None alone produces a ChatGPT.

歷史

為什麼 2017 年的 Transformer 是 AI 的「分水嶺」？

在 2017 年之前，處理語言的主流是 RNN / LSTM（一個字一個字依序看）。問題有三：(1) 無法平行計算（必須等前一個字算完）；(2) 長距離依賴衰減（一段話 20 個字後，開頭的資訊就被「遺忘」）；(3) GPU 利用率低，scale 不上去。

2017 年 6 月 Vaswani 等八人在 Google Brain 投出「Attention is All You Need」，提出 純 attention 架構：每個位置可以一次看到整句、所有 pair 都平行算。這直接解鎖了「scaling law」（Kaplan 2020、Hoffmann 2022 Chinchilla）——資料越多、模型越大、loss 越低；2018 GPT-1 → 2019 GPT-2 → 2020 GPT-3 → 2022 ChatGPT → 2024 Claude 3 → 2025 GPT-5。所有現代 LLM 都還是這個架構的後代。

對研究者的啟示是：技術跳變很少由「比較好調參」帶來，多半由「讓 scaling 變可能的架構改變」帶來。AlphaFold 2、scGPT、ESM-3 之所以可能，都依賴 Transformer 這層基建。

Before 2017, language was dominated by RNN / LSTM models (one token at a time). Three pain points: (1) no parallelism (must wait for the prior token); (2) long-range decay (information from 20 tokens back is "forgotten"); (3) low GPU utilization, can't scale.

In June 2017, eight Google Brain authors submitted "Attention is All You Need," proposing a pure-attention architecture: every position sees the entire sentence at once, and all pairs compute in parallel. This single move unlocked scaling laws (Kaplan 2020, Hoffmann 2022 Chinchilla) — more data + bigger model = lower loss. From 2018 GPT-1 → GPT-2 → GPT-3 → 2022 ChatGPT → 2024 Claude 3 → 2025 GPT-5. Every modern LLM is a descendant.

The research lesson: real jumps in capability rarely come from "better tuning." They come from architectural shifts that make scaling possible. AlphaFold 2, scGPT, and ESM-3 are all downstream of this Transformer foundation.

真實案例

兩個真實的生醫 AI 故事

🩺案例 A：Eric Topol 與 GPT-4 在心電圖解讀的開放實驗

Eric Topol（Scripps 創新主任、《Deep Medicine》作者）在 2023–2024 年多次公開分享他把單張心電圖丟給 GPT-4 / GPT-4V，請它先給「3 個最可能的 differential diagnosis + 各自的支持/反對證據」。多次測試中，模型在「AV block、QT 延長、LBBB、心房顫動」等典型 pattern 上判斷準確率與資深心臟科住院醫師相當；但在「電軸偏移 + 結構性心臟病的合併判讀」上常常給出「教科書式但臨床無關的」答案。他的結論是：AI 不是要取代心臟科醫師，是把住院醫師「升級」成資深心臟科的速度加倍。重點是醫師依然下最終判斷，並且要學會「會問模型對的問題」。這個案例後來被收進他在 Nature Medicine 與多場演講中「AI as a co-pilot, not autopilot」的論述核心。

Eric Topol (Scripps Translational, author of Deep Medicine) repeatedly shared, throughout 2023–2024, an open experiment: he fed single 12-lead ECG images to GPT-4 / GPT-4V and asked for "the three most likely differential diagnoses with supporting and opposing evidence." Across many cases, the model matched senior cardiology residents on classic patterns (AV block, prolonged QT, LBBB, atrial fibrillation) but produced "textbook-but-clinically-irrelevant" answers when axis deviation combined with structural disease. His conclusion: AI does not replace cardiologists — it doubles the rate at which residents grow into seniors. The clinician still decides; the trick is "asking the model the right question." This case became central to his Nature Medicine essays and talks framing AI as "co-pilot, not autopilot."

🧬案例 B：DeepMind AlphaFold 2 釋出 2 億+ 結構徹底改寫結構生物學

2020 年 DeepMind 在 CASP14 用 AlphaFold 2 把蛋白結構預測的 GDT_TS 推到接近實驗解析度（中位 92），震驚整個結構生物學社群。2021–2022 年他們把模型開源並與 EMBL-EBI 合作建立 AlphaFold Protein Structure Database，到 2024 年釋出超過 2 億個預測結構，涵蓋幾乎所有 UniProt 蛋白。這直接改變了多個學科的日常工作流：藥物設計可以從「等 X-ray 結晶」變成「先看 AlphaFold 結構」；微生物學者可以快速看冷門菌的 effector domain；某 PI 在實驗室會議告訴博後：「不要再花 6 個月做 crystal — 先看 AlphaFold，再決定值不值得做」。這個案例的教學意義是：一個 foundation model 釋出的結構資料庫，可以比任何單一發現更深遠地改變一個領域。2024 AlphaFold 3 進一步覆蓋蛋白-蛋白-小分子-核酸交互作用，繼續擴大這個版圖。

At CASP14 in 2020, DeepMind's AlphaFold 2 pushed structure prediction's median GDT_TS into the experimental range (~92), shocking the structural-biology community. In 2021–2022 they open-sourced the model and partnered with EMBL-EBI to build the AlphaFold Protein Structure Database, releasing more than 200 million predicted structures by 2024 — covering nearly all of UniProt. Daily workflows shifted overnight: drug design now starts with "look up AlphaFold first"; microbiologists profile obscure effector domains in minutes; one PI famously told a postdoc, "Don't spend six months on crystals — check AlphaFold first." The teaching point: a single foundation-model-derived database can reshape a field more than any one paper ever could. AlphaFold 3 (2024) extended this to protein–protein–ligand–nucleic-acid complexes.

常見誤區

新手最常踩的 6 個坑

  1. 把 LLM 當成「會記事實的資料庫」——它記的是 token co-occurrence，不是 fact，所以會自信地給你假 PMID。
  2. 覺得「模型越大越好」——許多日常任務（grammar fix、JSON 抽取）小模型已經足夠，省 10× 成本。
  3. 把整個 1 GB BAM 檔貼進 chat——LLM 不會 parse binary；先 sample / 統計再丟。
  4. 同一個 prompt 連問三次得不同答案就崩潰——這是 stochastic sampling，不是 bug。要重現請把 temperature=0 或固定 seed。
  5. 不分敏感資料就丟到 chatgpt.com——PHI / 病人資料必須走 BAA 或 on-prem，這是 HIPAA 紅線。
  6. 盲信「temperature=0 就不會錯」——它只是讓輸出穩定，不會讓 fact 變對；幻覺與 temperature 弱相關。
  1. Treating the LLM as a factual database — it stores token co-occurrence, not facts, so it confidently fabricates PMIDs.
  2. "Bigger is always better" — many daily tasks (grammar fix, JSON extraction) run fine on small models at 10× lower cost.
  3. Pasting a 1 GB BAM into chat — LLMs can't parse binary; sample or summarize first.
  4. Panicking that three identical prompts give three different answers — that's stochastic sampling, not a bug. Set temperature = 0 (or fix a seed) to reproduce.
  5. Dropping PHI into chatgpt.com — patient data must go through BAA or on-prem. HIPAA red line.
  6. Believing "temperature = 0 means no errors" — it only stabilizes output. Hallucinations are only weakly tied to temperature.
生統數理視角

給統計背景讀者的進階註腳

① Softmax 與 temperature 的數學形式。對 logits z = (z_1, …, z_V)（V 為詞彙量），帶 temperature T 的機率為 p_i(T) = exp(z_i / T) / Σ_j exp(z_j / T)。T → 0 退化為 argmax；T → ∞ 退化為均勻分布。當 T = 1 即一般 softmax。Temperature 不改變排序，只改變「熵」——低 T 熵小，輸出可預測；高 T 熵大，輸出多樣。可視為 softmax 後的溫度調整 Gibbs 分布，跟模擬退火同源。

② Cross-entropy loss 與 perplexity。訓練 LLM 的目標函數是負對數似然 L = − (1/N) · Σ log p(x_t | x_<t)。Perplexity = exp(L)，可理解為「模型在每一步平均要從幾個 token 中猶豫」。GPT-3 在 The Pile 上 PPL ≈ 9，現代 frontier 大致 3–5。

③ Scaling law 的統計學詮釋。Kaplan 2020 與 Chinchilla（Hoffmann 2022）顯示，loss L 與計算量 C、資料量 D、參數量 N 之間在對數尺度上近似冪律：L(N,D) ≈ A · N^(-α) + B · D^(-β) + L_∞。可視為一個三變量的非線性回歸，且訓練成本 C ≈ 6 · N · D 是強約束。「最佳 token 數 ≈ 20 倍參數量」這個經驗法則就是從這個拉格朗日最佳化推出來的。

④ Bayesian 視角。把 LLM 看作 p(output | prompt, θ)，prompt 工程其實是「在改 prior 與條件分布」。RLHF 則是用偏好資料移動 posterior，DPO（Rafailov 2023）證明可以直接最大化偏好對的似然，省去 PPO 的價值函數。

① Softmax with temperature. For logits z = (z_1, …, z_V), the temperature-T probabilities are p_i(T) = exp(z_i / T) / Σ_j exp(z_j / T). T → 0 collapses to argmax; T → ∞ becomes uniform. Temperature does not change ranking, only entropy. This is the Gibbs distribution with inverse-temperature 1/T — same family as simulated annealing.

② Cross-entropy loss and perplexity. LLM training minimizes negative log-likelihood L = − (1/N) · Σ log p(x_t | x_<t). Perplexity = exp(L): "how many tokens is the model effectively hesitating between at each step?" GPT-3 on The Pile ≈ 9; frontier models today ≈ 3–5.

③ Scaling laws as nonlinear regression. Kaplan 2020 and Chinchilla (Hoffmann 2022) found loss follows an approximate power law in compute C, data D, and parameters N: L(N,D) ≈ A · N^(-α) + B · D^(-β) + L_∞, with the strong constraint C ≈ 6 · N · D. The "≈ 20 tokens per parameter" heuristic comes from the Lagrangian optimum.

④ Bayesian framing. Treat an LLM as p(output | prompt, θ). Prompt engineering is "changing the conditioning"; RLHF moves the posterior with preference data; DPO (Rafailov 2023) skips PPO's value function by maximizing preference-pair likelihood directly.

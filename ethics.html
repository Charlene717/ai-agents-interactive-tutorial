學術倫理與期刊政策

ICMJE、Nature、Science、Cell——四套規則四個語氣，但有一條鐵律：人類作者負全責。

ICMJE, Nature, Science, Cell — four rulesets, four tones, one iron rule: human authors bear full responsibility.

術語速查

學術倫理必懂十二詞

從 ICMJE / CRediT 到撤稿 / predatory journal，這 12 個詞建立學術倫理的共同詞彙。在 AI 加入後，每個詞都被重新定義一次。

From ICMJE / CRediT to retraction / predatory journals, these 12 terms form the shared vocabulary of research ethics. With AI, each one has been redefined.

📜

ICMJE

International Committee of Medical Journal Editors。1978 創立，制定全球臨床醫學期刊作者署名、揭露、利益衝突標準。2023/8 公佈 AI 使用三鐵律；2025 修訂揭露範本更明確。

International Committee of Medical Journal Editors. Founded 1978, sets global standards for authorship, disclosure, and conflicts of interest in clinical medicine journals. Published the three AI rules in Aug 2023; refined disclosure templates in 2025.

✍️

Authorship

ICMJE 四標準：(1) 實質貢獻概念 / 設計 / 取得分析資料；(2) 草擬或實質修改；(3) 同意最終版本；(4) 對所有面向負責。AI 不能滿足 (4) → 不可掛名。

The ICMJE four criteria: (1) substantial contributions to conception / design / acquisition or analysis; (2) drafting or substantial revising; (3) final approval; (4) accountability for all aspects. AI cannot satisfy (4) → cannot be an author.

🙏

Acknowledgments

致謝段落。對未達 authorship 標準但有貢獻的個人 / AI / 機構列名。ICMJE 2023 起要求 AI 工具與用途列在 Acknowledgments + Methods 雙處。

Acknowledgements section. Lists individuals / AI / institutions that contributed but don't meet authorship criteria. ICMJE since 2023 requires AI tools and their use listed in both Acknowledgments and Methods.

📢

Disclosure

主動揭露所有可能影響研究 / 報導的因素（資金、COI、AI 使用）。AI 揭露至少包含：model name + version + cutoff date + 用途 + 範例 prompt（如非僅 copy-editing）。

Proactive disclosure of all factors potentially affecting research / reporting (funding, COI, AI use). AI disclosure must include: model name + version + cutoff date + purpose + sample prompt (unless purely copy-editing).

🎭

CRediT

Contributor Roles Taxonomy（NISO ANSI/NISO Z39.104-2022）。14 個標準化角色（Conceptualization、Methodology、Software、Validation 等）。AI 不可列在 CRediT 中——它不是 contributor。

Contributor Roles Taxonomy (NISO ANSI/NISO Z39.104-2022). 14 standardised roles (Conceptualization, Methodology, Software, Validation, etc.). AI cannot appear in CRediT — it isn't a contributor.

💼

Conflict of Interest

財務、個人、學術上可能影響判斷的關係。AI 廠商 advisory board、stock options 都需揭露。LLM 不能簽 COI 是它不能當作者的核心理由。

Financial, personal, or academic relationships that could bias judgement. AI vendor advisory boards and stock options must be disclosed. The fact that LLMs cannot sign a COI statement is the central reason they cannot be authors.

📑

Plagiarism

未經適當引用地使用他人文字 / idea。AI 改寫的「自我抄襲」與「unintended paraphrase plagiarism」是 2024+ 新興問題。iThenticate / Turnitin 已加入 AI-detected 檢測。

Using others' words or ideas without proper attribution. "Self-plagiarism" via AI rewriting and "unintended paraphrase plagiarism" are new 2024+ concerns. iThenticate / Turnitin now include AI-detection.

⛔

Retraction

期刊正式撤稿。原因含 fabrication / falsification / plagiarism / AI 圖像未揭露。2024 Frontiers 撤回多篇 AI 圖像有 nonsense 文字的論文。Retraction Watch 追蹤所有公開撤稿。

Formal journal retraction. Reasons include fabrication / falsification / plagiarism / undisclosed AI images. Frontiers retracted multiple papers with nonsense-text AI images in 2024. Retraction Watch tracks all public retractions.

🚫

Predatory Journal

收 APC 但無實質 peer review 的期刊。Beall's List → 2017 關閉 → 現有 Cabells Predatory Reports（付費）、DOAJ 白名單。AI 使年產假論文工廠論文激增——稱 "paper mill"。

Journals that charge APCs without real peer review. Beall's List → closed 2017 → now Cabells Predatory Reports (paid) and DOAJ whitelist. AI has accelerated paper-mill output massively.

👥

Peer Review

由領域同儕審查稿件。ICMJE / COPE 規定：審稿人不可將 manuscript 內容輸入公開 AI 工具（資料外洩 + 違反 confidentiality）。Springer Nature 2024 明確禁止。

Domain experts review manuscripts. ICMJE / COPE rule: reviewers must not paste manuscript content into public AI tools (data leakage + confidentiality breach). Springer Nature 2024 explicitly prohibits this.

🎲

Data Fabrication

偽造不存在的資料。AI 使「合成 plausible 看似真實但虛假的 raw data」變得容易。Bik 2024 警告 AI 生成的 Western blot / IHC 影像偵測極困難。

Inventing data that doesn't exist. AI makes it easy to synthesise plausible-looking but fake raw data. Bik 2024 warned that AI-generated Western blot / IHC images are extremely hard to detect.

🖼️

Image Manipulation

不當修改 image（splice、duplicate、enhance）。Cell Press / Nature 用 Proofig / Imagetwin 等 AI 工具偵測。AI 生成 (vs manipulated) 圖像在 2024 後成為新類別，多數頂刊禁止。

Improper image modification (splice, duplicate, enhance). Cell Press / Nature use AI tools like Proofig / Imagetwin for detection. AI-generated (vs manipulated) images became a new category post-2024 and are banned at most top journals.

概覽

為什麼 AI 政策變動如此頻繁？

2023–2026 期間，幾乎每家主流期刊都至少更新過兩次 AI 政策。最新（2025–2026）共識是 ICMJE 訂下的三條鐵律：(1) AI 不可掛名作者——因為它無法承擔內容責任、無法簽署 conflict of interest；(2) 必須在 Methods 揭露所有 LLM 使用——包含模型版本、用途、prompt 範例（除非僅做拼字 / 文法 copy-editing）；(3) 人類作者對所有內容（包括 AI 產出）負全責。

但各刊細則差異顯著：Nature / Springer Nature 禁止 AI 生成的圖像與影片；Science 起初最嚴（未經編輯同意完全禁用），2024 略放寬；Cell Press 提供標準化揭露範本；ICMJE 是整套臨床醫學期刊通用底線。違規後果從更正、撤稿到列入學術不端記錄。

From 2023 to 2026, almost every major journal updated its AI policy at least twice. The 2025–2026 consensus is the three ICMJE rules: (1) AI cannot be an author — it cannot take responsibility for content nor sign a conflict-of-interest statement; (2) Disclose all LLM use in Methods — including model version, purpose, sample prompts (unless purely copy-editing); (3) Human authors bear full responsibility for everything, including AI output.

Per-journal differences are real: Nature / Springer Nature bans AI-generated images and video; Science started strictest (a flat ban without editor approval) and relaxed slightly in 2024; Cell Press provides standardised disclosure templates; ICMJE is the floor for clinical medicine journals. Consequences range from corrections and retractions to formal misconduct records.

💡
核心心法：「揭露 + 人類負責」是 2026 的雙鐵律。任何時候用 AI 改寫、生成草稿、做分析，先想：(a) 我要寫進 Methods 嗎？(b) 期刊禁止這個用法嗎？(c) 我能不能為這段話的每個字負責？任何一個「不確定」，先停下來查官方政策。 Core mindset: "Disclose + human accountability" is the 2026 iron rule. Whenever you use AI to rewrite, draft, or analyse, ask: (a) Will I report this in Methods? (b) Does the journal forbid this use? (c) Can I stand behind every word? Any uncertainty → stop and read the official policy.
三條鐵律

一、ICMJE 2025 的三條鐵律

🚫

① AI 不可掛名作者

ChatGPT、Claude、Gemini 都不可列在 author list；它們無法承擔內容責任、無法簽 COI。

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini cannot be listed as authors; they cannot take responsibility for content nor sign COI declarations.

📋

② Methods 必須揭露

除非僅做拼字 / 文法 copy-editing，所有 LLM 使用都要在 Methods 揭露：模型版本、用途、prompt。

Unless purely copy-editing, all LLM use must be disclosed in Methods: model version, purpose, prompts.

⚖️

③ 人類作者負全責

所有內容——包括 AI 產出——人類作者必須驗證、為其準確性與完整性負責。

All content — including AI-generated text — must be verified by human authors who are accountable for accuracy and integrity.

五、真實案例與你的決策流程

2023–2025 已有多起公開案例：(1) 一篇 PLOS 期刊論文因為 AI 生成的解剖學圖示（鼠蛋蛋過度誇張）被撤稿；(2) 多篇引用「不存在的 PMID」被讀者抓出（hallucinated reference），導致更正或撤稿；(3) 一位審稿人把稿件貼進 ChatGPT 索取 review 草稿，被作者透過浮水印偵測到，編輯撤換審稿人並列入紀錄。

Real cases from 2023–2025: (1) A PLOS paper retracted because of an AI-generated anatomical figure (notoriously exaggerated rodent anatomy); (2) Multiple papers caught with "non-existent PMID" citations (hallucinated references), leading to corrections or retractions; (3) A reviewer pasted a manuscript into ChatGPT for a draft review and was caught via watermarking — editor replaced the reviewer and logged the breach.

使用 AI 寫稿前的 3 個自問

Q1→
這個用法該期刊允許嗎？查最新政策（Nature 禁圖、Science 嚴格、ICMJE 是底線）。
Does the journal allow this use? Check the latest policy (Nature bans images, Science is strict, ICMJE is the floor).
Q2→
我能在 Methods 寫清楚 model + 用途 + prompt 嗎？能 → OK；不能 / 不想 → 不要用。
Can I clearly state model + purpose + prompt in Methods? Yes → OK; No / unwilling → don't use it.
Q3→

真實案例

兩個深入案例

🖼️

案例 1：Frontiers 2024 「老鼠 nonsense 圖」撤稿事件

2024 年 2 月 13 日，Frontiers in Cell and Developmental Biology 發表一篇關於 stem cell 訊息傳遞的論文，包含一張「老鼠解剖」示意圖——但圖中老鼠擁有四個解剖位置錯誤、字形怪異無意義的標籤，包括 "dissilced"、"Stemm cells" 等明顯 AI 生成 garbage text，且老鼠生殖器尺寸不合解剖比例。社群媒體（X / 前 Twitter）在 24 小時內瘋傳，超過 1.2 萬次轉發，The Verge、Ars Technica、Nature News 都做新聞。Frontiers 在 48 小時內撤稿，並承認：(a) 審稿人發現了圖中問題並要求修正，但作者未實質修改；(b) 編輯部最終決定接受時未複查圖像；(c) 圖片產生器是 Midjourney，作者在 Methods 揭露為 "Figure generated by Midjourney" 但編輯部未認知此為禁止內容。事件導致 Frontiers 立即更新政策：所有 AI 生成圖像 ban；強制使用 Proofig AI 圖像偵測；對所有曾接受論文 retrospective audit。對學術界的衝擊：證明 (1) AI 圖像偵測必須自動化、人類審稿人會漏；(2) Methods 揭露不等於同意——某些用法即使揭露也禁止；(3) 「以為審稿人會抓」是極危險的偏誤。Bik 2024 警告：更精緻的 AI 生成 Western blot / IHC 圖像可能已悄悄通過審查，現有 retraction watch 只是冰山一角。

Case 1: The Frontiers 2024 "nonsense rat" retraction

On 13 Feb 2024, Frontiers in Cell and Developmental Biology published a paper on stem-cell signalling with a rat anatomy schematic — except the rat had four anatomically wrong labels in garbled non-words ("dissilced," "Stemm cells") and impossible genital proportions. Social media (X / former Twitter) blew up in 24 hours with 12K+ reshares; The Verge, Ars Technica, and Nature News all covered it. Frontiers retracted within 48 hours and admitted: (a) reviewers had flagged the figure and requested revisions but the authors didn't materially fix it; (b) editorial acceptance didn't re-check the image; (c) the image generator was Midjourney, disclosed in Methods as "Figure generated by Midjourney" but the editorial office didn't recognise this as banned content. The event forced Frontiers to immediately update policy: ban all AI-generated images, require Proofig AI image detection, and retrospectively audit prior accepted papers. Impact on academia: (1) AI-image detection must be automated — human reviewers will miss; (2) Methods disclosure ≠ permission — some uses are banned even when disclosed; (3) "reviewers will catch it" is a dangerous illusion. Bik 2024 warned that more polished AI-generated Western blot / IHC images may have already slipped through review — current retraction lists are the tip of the iceberg.

📨

案例 2：ICMJE 2025 修訂後某 NEJM-class 期刊退稿流程

2025 年 ICMJE 修訂揭露範本變嚴後，某 NEJM-class 期刊（為討論方便稱 "Journal X"）發生以下真實流程：投稿一篇 cardiology RCT 二次分析。作者 Methods 寫「We used AI for writing assistance」，cover letter 提到「used ChatGPT」，但未指明 model version、cutoff、用途與範圍。Editorial 在 desk-review 階段以下三點退稿（72 小時內）：(1) 揭露不足：未指明 GPT-4 / GPT-5、未列 cutoff date、未說明用於 Methods 改寫還是 Discussion 草擬；(2) 引用驗證缺失：抽查 Reference list 12 條，發現 3 條 PMID 對應不同論文（明顯 hallucinated）；(3) Acknowledgments 未列 AI：違反 ICMJE 2023 雙處揭露要求。Editorial decision 為「Reject — major integrity concerns」，並附建議：若想 resubmit 需 (a) 完整揭露含 model + version + cutoff + prompt template + 章節範圍；(b) 提供 citation verification log（每筆 PMID/DOI 驗證 timestamp）；(c) Acknowledgments 補上 AI tool + 用途；(d) 接受 image manipulation AI check（Proofig）。作者最終 8 週後完整補全 resubmit，二輪 peer review 後接受。關鍵 takeaway：Journal-side enforcement 正在加緊，「used AI for writing」這種模糊揭露 2025 後等於沒揭露；honest + complete 揭露反而是最快路徑。

Case 2: An NEJM-class journal desk-reject after the ICMJE 2025 revision

After ICMJE tightened its disclosure template in 2025, an NEJM-class journal (call it "Journal X") ran this real workflow: a submission of a secondary analysis of a cardiology RCT. The Methods said "We used AI for writing assistance"; the cover letter mentioned "used ChatGPT," but neither specified model version, cutoff, purpose, or scope. Editorial desk-rejected within 72 hours on three grounds: (1) insufficient disclosure — no GPT-4 / GPT-5 version, no cutoff date, no scope between Methods rewrite and Discussion drafting; (2) missing citation verification — random check of 12 references found 3 PMIDs resolving to different papers (clearly hallucinated); (3) Acknowledgments missed AI — breached ICMJE 2023 double-disclosure rule. The editorial decision: "Reject — major integrity concerns" with conditions for resubmission: (a) full disclosure including model + version + cutoff + prompt template + section scope; (b) a citation-verification log (timestamps for every PMID / DOI); (c) AI tool + purpose added to Acknowledgments; (d) accept image-manipulation AI check (Proofig). The authors resubmitted 8 weeks later with everything closed; accepted after a second peer review. Key takeaway: journal-side enforcement is tightening — "used AI for writing" is effectively zero disclosure after 2025; complete honest disclosure is now the fastest path.

常見誤區

學術倫理 AI 揭露最常踩的五個坑

⚠️
誤區 1：Methods 揭露太籠統（「used AI for writing」）
為什麼錯：ICMJE 2023 + 2025 修訂明確要求揭露 model + version + cutoff + 用途 + 範圍。「used AI for writing」這種模糊文字 2025 後等於未揭露，多家頂刊已 desk-reject。Editorial 認為這暗示作者試圖隱藏實質用法。
正確做法：標準 Methods 範本「We used [GPT-5 / Claude 4.5 / Gemini 2.5 Pro] (model version YYYY-MM, knowledge cutoff YYYY-MM) for [purpose: e.g. Methods grammar polishing, Discussion paraphrasing, or first-draft Introduction]. All AI-suggested citations were manually verified via PubMed / Crossref on [date]. No AI was used for data analysis or figure generation.」
Pitfall 1: Methods disclosure too vague ("used AI for writing")
Why wrong: ICMJE 2023 + 2025 revisions explicitly require model + version + cutoff + purpose + scope. Vague phrasing like "used AI for writing" effectively counts as non-disclosure post-2025; multiple top journals desk-reject for it. Editors read it as concealment.
Fix: standard Methods template — "We used [GPT-5 / Claude 4.5 / Gemini 2.5 Pro] (model version YYYY-MM, knowledge cutoff YYYY-MM) for [purpose: Methods grammar polishing, Discussion paraphrasing, or first-draft Introduction]. All AI-suggested citations were manually verified via PubMed / Crossref on [date]. No AI was used for data analysis or figure generation."
⚠️
誤區 2：用 AI 做同儕審查
為什麼錯：把 manuscript 內容貼進公開 AI 工具 = 洩漏 unpublished data + 違反 confidentiality（COPE / ICMJE / Springer Nature 2024 明文禁止）。即使是 enterprise 版本，也可能違反 publisher 與 reviewer 的 NDA。後果：被列入學術不端紀錄、永久禁止為該期刊 review。
正確做法：peer review 全程不可用任何 LLM 工具（特例：publisher-provided + 已通知作者的工具）。寫 review 時 ChatGPT-Plus 等個人帳號禁用——即使是「協助寫英文」也不被允許。
Pitfall 2: Using AI for peer review
Why wrong: pasting manuscript content into public AI tools = leaking unpublished data + breaching confidentiality (explicitly forbidden by COPE / ICMJE / Springer Nature 2024). Even enterprise versions may breach publisher-reviewer NDAs. Consequences: misconduct record, permanent reviewer ban from that journal.
Fix: no LLM tools during peer review (exception: publisher-provided tools the authors have been notified about). Personal ChatGPT-Plus accounts are off-limits even "to polish my English" — not allowed.
⚠️
誤區 3：以為 copy-editing 免揭露，但實際用了重寫
為什麼錯：ICMJE 與多數期刊對純拼字 / 文法 copy-editing 確實 exempt，但門檻很窄——只指「不改變語意、不增刪句子」。若用 AI「rewrite for clarity」、「improve flow」、「reorganize paragraph」就已超出 copy-editing 範圍，必揭露。作者常自我安慰「只是改流暢度」，實際 30%+ wording 被替換。
正確做法：保留 before / after diff（Git 或 track changes）；超過 ~5% 字句變動 + 任何句子重組 / 增刪 = 揭露。寧過頭也別漏。
Pitfall 3: Believing copy-editing is exempt while actually rewriting
Why wrong: ICMJE and most journals exempt pure spelling / grammar copy-editing, but the threshold is narrow — "no semantic change, no add / remove sentences." Using AI to "rewrite for clarity," "improve flow," or "reorganize paragraphs" exceeds copy-editing and must be disclosed. Authors comfort themselves with "just polishing flow" while 30%+ wording is replaced.
Fix: keep before / after diffs (Git or track changes); >~5% wording change + any sentence reorg / add-delete = disclose. Over-disclose rather than under-disclose.
⚠️
誤區 4：用 AI 生成 figure 但不揭露
為什麼錯：Nature / Springer Nature / Cell Press / Science 對 AI 生成圖像皆已明文禁止，即使揭露也不接受。AI 改 schematic 或繪 conceptual figure 看似無害，但模型可能加入 nonsense detail（Frontiers 老鼠事件即是）。Proofig / Imagetwin 等 AI 偵測工具已能識別 90%+ AI 生成圖像，2025 多家期刊強制使用。
正確做法：所有 figure 用傳統工具（BioRender、Adobe Illustrator、PowerPoint）；如必要使用 AI（如 ChemDraw AI assist），需明確揭露並提供原稿；對 schematic 必人工審核每個 label 與 element。
Pitfall 4: Using AI to generate figures without disclosure
Why wrong: Nature / Springer Nature / Cell Press / Science explicitly ban AI-generated images — disclosure does not help. AI-drawn schematics or conceptual figures look harmless but models inject nonsense details (the Frontiers rat case). Proofig / Imagetwin detect 90%+ of AI-generated images; many journals require these checks in 2025.
Fix: all figures via traditional tools (BioRender, Adobe Illustrator, PowerPoint). If AI must be used (e.g. ChemDraw AI assist), disclose explicitly and provide the raw output; manually review every label and element on every schematic.
⚠️
誤區 5：Cover letter 主動承認 AI 用法但 Methods 沒寫
為什麼錯：揭露不一致 = inconsistent disclosure → editorial 視為紅旗。若 cover letter 寫「used ChatGPT for writing」但 Methods 與 Acknowledgments 皆無提，editorial 會懷疑作者試圖只在 informal 層級揭露而不留正式紀錄。ICMJE 2023 雙處揭露要求即為防止此情況。
正確做法：揭露必三處一致——Methods + Acknowledgments + Cover letter，內容相同。Cover letter 不可比 Methods 多 / 少資訊；如有 funding 與 COI，三處皆需。
Pitfall 5: Cover letter admits AI use but Methods doesn't
Why wrong: inconsistent disclosure = red flag to editorial. If the cover letter says "used ChatGPT for writing" but Methods and Acknowledgments are silent, editors suspect authors are only disclosing informally to avoid the formal record. The ICMJE 2023 double-disclosure rule exists precisely to prevent this.
Fix: disclosure must be consistent across Methods + Acknowledgments + Cover letter, identical wording. The cover letter must not contain more / less than Methods; funding and COI follow the same three-way rule.

進階細節

🔬 Liang 2024 AI 偵測、ICMJE 範本標準化、Nature 圖像禁令的著作權邏輯、SN 政策時間軸

① Liang 2024 偵測 AI-modified content。Liang et al. 2024（arXiv:2403.07183）用 word-frequency shift（"delve"、"meticulous"、"showcasing" 等詞在 2023+ 論文急升）+ stylistic embedding distance（與 LLM-generated reference 的相似度）估計 17.5% 計算機科學摘要、6.3% 自然科學摘要在 2023/3 後「實質受 ChatGPT 修改」。方法：把 ICLR / NeurIPS / Nature 摘要的 word-frequency 與 2022 比較，計算偏離 baseline 的 expected ratio，假設這些偏離主因是 LLM 改寫。2024 Liang et al. 在 arXiv 後續論文延伸到 peer review 偵測，發現 ~17% NeurIPS reviews 顯示 LLM 寫作 signature。限制：population-level signal，個別論文無法 reliably 判定；human-edited AI output 可能 evade detection。但這提供 publisher 一個 aggregate level 監控指標。

② ICMJE 揭露範本標準化（2023 vs 2025）。2023/8 ICMJE 公佈三鐵律：(a) AI 不可掛名；(b) Methods 揭露 AI tool + 用途；(c) 人類作者負全責。2025 修訂進一步要求：(d) 揭露 model version + cutoff date；(e) Methods + Acknowledgments + Cover letter 三處一致；(f) AI 用於 reference 必附 verification log。各 section 範本：Methods「The authors used [tool, version, date] for [purpose: e.g., language editing of Methods Section; first-draft generation of Discussion section]. Authors verified all factual content and citations.」Acknowledgments「[Tool name] was used to [purpose].」Cover letter「In compliance with ICMJE 2023 + 2025, we disclose AI tool use as described in Methods.」

③ Nature 圖像禁令的著作權邏輯。Nature / Springer Nature 2023 政策「ban AI-generated images」並非單純科學誠信考量，而是著作權法（copyright）邏輯：US Copyright Office 2023/3 在 Zarya of the Dawn（Kashtanova v. USCO）裁定 AI 生成內容「lacks human authorship」不享 copyright protection。Nature 期刊內容必須能 enforce 著作權（轉載、引用、reuse），若 AI 圖像 unprotectable 則 publisher 無法控制使用。例外：human-significant-modification 後的 AI 圖像可能可獲 copyright（USCO 2023 一案中對「human modify + selection」部分給予 protection）。所以即使 Nature 禁止，Springer Nature 在 Cell-like 旗下某些非 flagship 期刊曾考慮接受 human-significantly-modified AI 圖像，但 2024 後統一禁止以簡化政策。EU 著作權邏輯類似但不完全相同——EUIPO 2024 報告中對 AI-assisted 與 AI-generated 區分得更細。

④ Springer Nature AI 政策時間軸。2023/1：ChatGPT 不可掛名（首家頂刊明文禁止），LLM 用法須揭露。2023/6：禁止 AI 生成 / AI 改造 image / video。2023/11：明確 peer review 不可用公開 LLM 工具。2024/3：強制使用 Proofig / Imagetwin 對 figure 自動 AI 偵測。2024/9：Methods + Acknowledgments + cover letter 三處揭露要求。2025/2：揭露範本更新含 model version + cutoff。2025/8：開始 retrospective audit 2023+ 接受論文（抽樣 ~1%）。Cell Press 與 ACS 採近似時間軸；Science 走較嚴格路線（2023 完全禁，2024 略放寬但仍要 editorial pre-approval）。

① Liang 2024 — detecting AI-modified content. Liang et al. 2024 (arXiv:2403.07183) used word-frequency shifts ("delve," "meticulous," "showcasing" spiking in 2023+ papers) plus stylistic embedding distance to LLM-generated references, estimating 17.5% of CS abstracts and 6.3% of natural-science abstracts post-March 2023 are "substantially modified by ChatGPT." Method: compare ICLR / NeurIPS / Nature abstract word frequencies to 2022 baseline; treat deviation as a population-level LLM signal. A follow-up paper extends this to peer review, finding ~17% of NeurIPS reviews carry LLM signatures. Limits: a population-level signal — individual papers cannot be reliably classified; human-edited AI output evades detection. But it gives publishers an aggregate-level monitoring metric.

② ICMJE disclosure templates (2023 vs 2025). Aug 2023 ICMJE issued three iron rules: (a) AI cannot be an author; (b) Methods discloses AI tool + purpose; (c) human authors bear full responsibility. The 2025 revision adds: (d) disclose model version + cutoff date; (e) Methods + Acknowledgments + Cover letter must agree; (f) AI-suggested references require a verification log. Templates: Methods "The authors used [tool, version, date] for [purpose: e.g., language editing of Methods; first-draft Discussion]. Authors verified all factual content and citations." Acknowledgments "[Tool name] was used to [purpose]." Cover letter "In compliance with ICMJE 2023 + 2025, we disclose AI tool use as described in Methods."

③ Nature image-ban copyright logic. The Nature / Springer Nature 2023 ban on AI-generated images isn't purely about scientific integrity — it's also copyright law. The US Copyright Office in March 2023 (Zarya of the Dawn, Kashtanova v. USCO) ruled AI-generated content "lacks human authorship" and gets no copyright protection. Nature content must be enforceable for reproduction / citation / reuse; if AI images are unprotectable, the publisher cannot control downstream use. Exception: AI images with substantial human modification may earn copyright (USCO 2023 granted protection for the human "selection + arrangement"). So some non-flagship Springer Nature journals briefly considered accepting human-significantly-modified AI images, but unified to a ban in 2024 for simplicity. EU copyright logic is similar — EUIPO 2024 distinguishes AI-assisted vs AI-generated more finely.

④ Springer Nature AI policy timeline. Jan 2023: ChatGPT can't be an author (first major publisher to explicitly forbid); LLM use must be disclosed. Jun 2023: ban on AI-generated / AI-altered images / video. Nov 2023: peer review cannot use public LLM tools. Mar 2024: mandatory Proofig / Imagetwin AI-image detection on figures. Sep 2024: triple disclosure (Methods + Acknowledgments + cover letter). Feb 2025: disclosure template updated with model version + cutoff. Aug 2025: retrospective audit of 2023+ accepted papers (~1% sample). Cell Press and ACS track a similar timeline; Science is stricter (2023 full ban, 2024 relaxed slightly but still needs editorial pre-approval).

討論問題

與你的 lab 思辨這四題

Q1: 實習生 cover letter 完全用 AI 寫該揭露嗎？

實習生在投稿時用 ChatGPT 寫 cover letter（不是 Methods 也不是 manuscript 本體）。某 PI 認為「cover letter 是 communication，免揭露」；另一派認為「cover letter 是稿件正式部分，需揭露」。ICMJE 規定不明確。請思考：你支持哪邊？如果以「凡涉及對 editorial 的呈現都要揭露」為原則，是否該對致謝信、rebuttal letter、回應審稿意見都揭露？這會不會反而 punish 誠實的非英文母語者？

Q1: Should a trainee disclose using AI for the cover letter?

A trainee uses ChatGPT to draft a cover letter (not Methods, not manuscript body). One PI says "the cover letter is communication — no disclosure needed"; another says "the cover letter is a formal submission part — disclose." ICMJE is ambiguous. Consider: which camp? If the principle is "anything presented to editorial requires disclosure," should rebuttal letters and review responses also be disclosed? Does this punish honest non-native speakers?

Q2: AI 生成 schematic 與 Photoshop figure manipulation 該等同處理嗎？

傳統 Photoshop splice / duplicate Western blot 是 misconduct；AI 生成 conceptual schematic（如示意 signaling pathway）目前多被禁止但「嚴重程度」尚有爭議。Frontiers 老鼠事件中作者「只是」想要漂亮 schematic，無 fabrication 意圖。請思考：你認為兩者應等同嚴重嗎？理由為何？如果 AI 生成 schematic 可被認定無誤導 intent，是否該降為「需揭露但允許」？對教育性 illustration（textbook chapter）是否該不同？

Q2: AI-generated schematics vs Photoshop figure manipulation — same severity?

Traditional Photoshop splice / duplicate of a Western blot is misconduct; AI-generated conceptual schematics (e.g. signalling diagrams) are currently banned but the severity is debated. In the Frontiers rat case, authors "just" wanted a pretty schematic, no fabrication intent. Consider: should they be treated as equally severe? Why? If AI schematics carry no misleading intent, should they drop to "must disclose, allowed"? Should educational illustrations (textbook chapters) be treated differently?

Q3: 非英文母語者用 AI 改 Methods，揭露不揭露公平嗎？

某非英文母語博後用 ChatGPT 修改 Methods 文法，因此揭露；其同事是 native speaker 自行修文，不揭露。Editorial 對前者投以「AI-assisted」標籤，對後者視為「正常稿件」。實質上兩者輸出品質可能類似，但 perception 不同。請思考：這算 disclosure penalty 嗎？該如何修正？是否應該對 native speaker 也要求列出 editing assistance？或對 AI 揭露採「neutral language」避免暗示低品質？

Q3: Is disclosure unfair to non-native English speakers using AI?

A non-native postdoc uses ChatGPT for Methods grammar, discloses it. A native-speaker peer self-edits, no disclosure. Editorial labels the former "AI-assisted" and treats the latter as "normal." Outputs may be similar quality but perceptions differ. Consider: is this a disclosure penalty? How to fix? Should native speakers also disclose editing assistance? Should AI disclosure use neutral language to avoid implying lower quality?

Q4: 期刊該強制公開所有揭露的 AI 使用嗎？

目前 AI 揭露雖寫進 Methods 但 reader 通常不會注意；某些 advocate 提議「在論文頂部置 AI Use Statement banner」如 retraction notice 般顯眼；反對者認為這 stigmatize 合理用法。請思考：你支持顯眼 banner 嗎？這會鼓勵 honest disclosure 還是反而促使作者隱藏？對 readership 透明度（讓 reader 自己判斷 AI 介入程度）與 author equity（避免「AI scarlet letter」）如何平衡？

Q4: Should journals prominently flag all AI use?

Currently AI disclosure lives in Methods and readers rarely notice; some advocates propose a prominent "AI Use Statement" banner atop each paper (like a retraction notice). Critics argue this stigmatises legitimate use. Consider: do you support visible banners? Does this encourage honest disclosure or push authors to hide? How do you balance reader transparency (let readers judge AI involvement) with author equity (avoid the "AI scarlet letter")?
